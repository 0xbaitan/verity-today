 HOZIER: AN OBSESSION


Rajkishore Mukherjee

Sept 1, 2020   |   Entertainment

""Speaking of this prolific identity, who rose to fame with his iconic "Take Me To Church" in 2014, Hozier is a person who never believed that his songs were made for the quintessential public. We hugely relate to the iconic "Take Me To Church" but little do we realise the magnanimous significance that the particular song holds. Gay rights and LGBT+ movements were raging all over Russia when "Take Me To Church" was penned down as a song. A noteworthy point here is that Hozier did not posthumously rise to fame after the song came out, but after a year, when the song gradually started appearing at different places, people could relate to the beautifully articulated emotions and within no time, Hozier was topping the charts everywhere."



Hozier was nothing more than an Irish weirdie dude, occasionally singing in the open mics around - pretty jovial along with his peers and then lo, he leads the overall English Pop scenario by having his own very individualistic soundscape which appeals to most of us.

But, the question here is - What does Hozier garner? What makes him possess such an amazing blend of an audience and at the same time - stay rooted in his forte of beautiful poetry and lyricism?

I can say that Hozier brings forth at least about 25 other emotions along with him in his lyrics but, that's just not the point. Coming to the overall musicality of Hozier, lyrics play a very important role in them and it is indeed very alluring to see such beautiful poetry in the 21st Century when everything is centred around EDM and whatnot.

"When I was a child, I'd sit for hours
Staring into open flame
Something in it had a power
Could barely tear my eyes away
All you have is your fire
And the place you need to reach

Don't you ever tame your demons
But always keep 'em on a leash
When I was 16, my senses fooled me
Thought gasoline was on my clothes
I knew that something would always rule me
I knew the scent was mine alone"



Pure art.I leave it to the readers to guess the song. One of my personal favourites

Coming to the context of melodic significance, Hozier is extremely versatile in that perspective. The fact that Hozier remains stationed in a singular genre is incorrect because, as I said Hozier brings numerous different emotions through his extremely diverse choice of the soundscape which we often hear through his music. Hozier commonly chooses genres such as Blues, Contemporary R & B, Indie Pop, Folk Music, Soul Music, Blues Rock and Indie Rock for his unearthly music.

WOW, that's quite a number but yet, it is extremely unsurprising because Hozier's construction of the sound is one of the most unique I have ever seen so far and hence, he remains to be one of my favourite artists.

"My lover's got humour, she's the giggle at a funeral" - Hozier
