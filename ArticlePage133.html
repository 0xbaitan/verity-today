 SATRAJIT SEN:INTERVIEW


Megha Lahiri

Sept 1, 2020   |   Entertainment

""A versatile artist, Satrajit Sen is a director, producer, and entrepreneur who primarily works in Bengali cinema. Satrajit has the distinction of being the only producer and director who has had a workshop experience at Lee Strasberg Institute. Having assisted Bill Hopkins, the chief assistant to acclaimed directors, Martin Scorsese and Clint Eastwood, he started his career in films as a producer. His 2014 production venture, "Bakita Byaktigato" won the National Award for Best Film. His maiden directorial venture is the 2018 film, Michael, a Bengali drama starring Mir Afsar Ali, Soumitra Chatterjee, Kanchan Mullick and Arunima Ghosh.



We would firstly love to know about your childhood


"I was born and brought up in Kolkata itself, studied at Dolna Day school till class 8 and then moved to the Assembly of God church. I eventually moved on to Asutosh college and graduated from there.."


Tell us about your journey in flim direction and production.


"I started off by forming a company named Tripod Entertainment with 3 childhood friends of mine.We started by picking up films which were stuck at various stages of production or completed and not released due to lack of funds. First film was Macch Mishti and More which we released and it was a very successful film in terms of the box office collections. it was directed by Mainak Bhaumick. My first film as a director was Michael in 2018 which was a satire and had an ensemble cast. I had done a stint in the USA at the prestigious Lee Strasberg Institute of Theatre and Films and even made my first short film in 2016 which was in English and shot entirely in NYC.."


How do you choose your actors? Do you write the scripts thinking about someone in mind or do you write the script first as we have heard Satyajit Ray wrote the Feluda series keeping in Soumitra Chattopadhyay in mind all along?


"I don't write my own scripts as I don't consider myself to be a good enough screenplay writer. I have a flair for writing though and sometimes the germ of a story forms in my mind which I pass on to my writer colleagues for further development. Most of the time our stories are of course written with the person in mind who would be playing the character on screen.."


Can you tell us a Little about your next project. ?


"Its an English feature film to be shot in Orlando -genre is Drama currently the project is currently at the script development stage.."


Tell us about one incident which will make the readers happy/surprised.


"In our industry there are so many of these incidents while we shoot. For example Soumitra Jethu was the main man in my film Michael and he doesn't like to give retakes so every time we had to coax a retake out of him it was always my AD Prasenjit who used to go up to him and make lame excuses such as the black cloth used for darkening the room had come in on the frame by mistake etc etc as an explanation for the retakes. He of course got our whole game at the end of the day, but let it pass I guess. Plus with Mir impersonating and mimicing everybody it was always a blast.."


I would love to ask you as to How you have been spending your time the last 5 months.


"So I consult for a production house apart form running my own and they are into producing daily soaps so since April itself we have been at work sending out budgets to the channels on which our shows run and casting for upcoming shows etc. In my spare time I was exercising, watching films and some series, playing the piano a bit and playing with our dog Teetu .."


Your advice to young children who also want to become flim directors.


"One advice I give to everyone -if you have decided on something then do not give up till you make that happen no matter what. It is never easy and will not be for you but you must go on and on till you achieve what you had set out to do..."
