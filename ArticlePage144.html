 Cal


@calcalcutta

Sept 1, 2020   |   ...

For those who don't know me, let's talk about myself. I'm Anubhav Dass and I'm 17. I've been an fashion enthusiast and thought of myself to be a creative, optimistic entrepreneur. I have always fostered the dream of having a fashion brand of my own someday. One fine day, I decided I want to give these ideas some shape. Thus, the thought struck me, why buy supreme when you can make your own Cal? Elon Musk said, “When something is important enough, you do it even if the odds are not in your favor.” People would often question my age but by the grace of God and support from a lot of people, my age didn’t create hindrance for me to start working on what I always envisioned.



We started in February 2020 and have been facing immense limitations ever since due to the COVID-19 situation. It's been a fun ride since the beginning. We've gained so much experience in such a brief period of time and there’s much more to come as we strive forward. I've been receiving job applications from several NIFT students showing their enthusiasm to join us and it really made us feel special and important, it showed they were approving of us, our style and fashion statements.

Our aim is to promote hip-hop fashion culture in India but in its very own way! The designs are bold and the people who wear them speak volumes about their ability to cross lines and break social stigmas. For long people have looked down upon the streetwear culture. Our dresses emphasise on gender fluidity too. We at Cal believe, streetwear is language. A less spoken one but an important one. Another major reason for starting Cal was to change the belief and concepts of people about how Indians don't like to experiment with their clothes. We plan to merge the “Desi” street fashion with global street fashion. If I'm to be honest, my inspirations are very diverse. When it comes to streetwear, it has to be virgil abloh, also I appreciate kidsuper a lot! He's been one of my idols. Both of their works are always out of box and special. Everybody's crazy about streetwear these days and it's always exciting when homegrown designers take the plunge to create independent streetwear labels. Not since the 1990s hip-hop scene have baggy clothes and loud prints been so cool, Cal has been a reimagination of the notorious big wardrobe. We even plan on merging retro style with street wear, as it is the retro fashion is making quite the comeback, be it polka dots or checks, the collection is yet to release albeit we are yearning to release it in a twinkling.



We understand how my style has always been very minimal and I've focused on the fact that fashion has no gender, so we mainly create genderless outfits. I also focus on accessories when it comes to styling for the drip we all want to flaunt. personalised pieces often acquire better sentimental value than other items thus we cater to that need of our clients too.

We've been experimenting with quirky fabrics like reflective, suede, corduroy and many more. We also have been blending desi fabrics like khadi, and gamcha with streetwear. The fusion has received immense love and a lot of support from people around, who understand the streetwear game. We support sustainable fashion thus we are trying to work more on recycling and using recycled fabric. We hope this venture proves to be fruitful and has immense effect in the industry!
