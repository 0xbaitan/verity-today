 Verity- Creativity


Ayushi Banerjee

September 1, 2020   |   Creativity

What art means to me is something I can never put into words. The bookish definition of art is “the expression or application of human creative skill and imagination, typically in a visual form”, but to me, art is so much more than just a “creative skill”. A word so small, ironically holds a much bigger meaning and importance in my life.

I never realized, when scribbling on a piece of paper with a crayon, attempting to draw a hut, turned into my passion and my biggest source of happiness. And, I definitely never would’ve thought that I’d create an art account. Was I nervous? Yes, I was, of course I was but at the end of the day it was all worth it.

One year ago when I created my account on Instagram, under @ayushilovesart, I was new to it all, I didn’t have a clue about the art community. I created an account only to share with people something that I love to do but it was only after I started getting familiar with everything that I realized that this platform was so much more than just sharing my artwork, this was a place where I could raise my voice and put forth my views on matters that oppose or stand for.

Not only did creating this account give me platform to raise my voice, it also helped me improve my work, it helped me find me style. With the help of some of the most amazing people, I met through this account who are members of the art community on Instagram, I was finally able do it all right. I mean earlier I just used to draw for fun, but now I was exposed to all the right materials, the correct techniques, basically important pointers that helped me make my art less childish and more realistic.

Creating an art account on Instagram is obviously toxic but if want to improve yourself and challenge yourself to do better, there is no other place that is gonna encourage you to do so. Seeing other artists grow and improve in their work gives you the motivation to do better too.



I’m gonna give a very simple example of how my style changed after creating this account. Earlier I did all my artwork using either charcoal or watercolor and no matter how much people told me my art was good, I never seemed to be satisfied with it. I always saw these artists create pieces which looked so realistic as though they were straight up printouts; and I always wanted that. Searching up for tutorials on YouTube wasn’t enough because most of the time they were in time-lapse and very difficult for beginners like me to understand. What helped me really improve my artwork was talking to artists on Instagram who were better than me and taking suggestions/tips from them. And I never thought it would work, but it did and I started creating the kind of art I used to admire. Within a year’s time there was drastic improvement in art form, don’t believe me? See for yourself:-



Through this account I have not only gained love and support from all of my followers but I have also received recognition from celebrities including Maggie Lindeman, Hannah stocking and Lele Pons.

I won’t lie by saying that it’s all perfect. Nothing is. As much fun as it is to create an account Instagram, it is just as much difficult to maintain it and sometimes it gets really toxic. There will be many people who will question your art but again, at the end of the day you have to be true to yourself and know who your real well-wishers are, who want the best for you and want your account to grow and who are just there to create chaos.

Though I was hesitant at first, creating this account was one of the best decisions that I made, because this account has been nothing but good to me. Starting from all the amazing people that I’ve come across through this account to the enormous amount of love and support I receive from my followers. This account made me love my work and made me way more confident than I used to be.
