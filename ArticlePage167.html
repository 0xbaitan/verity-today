 Opinion: HIGH SCHOOL IN MOVIES VS IN REAL LIFE


Ruchika Bhowsinghka

Dec 1, 2020   |   Education

Innumerable assignments, almost no free time, tests, and a huge amount of stress, these are an integral part of high school. Although, for some strange reason, most of the time the portrayal of high school in movies rarely contains any of these factors. High school is a complex experience for everyone. Students go through a lot of changes, ranging from physical to mental and even emotional.
In the case of adolescence and high school, movies and tv shows overdramatize high school life to make movies and TV more interesting to younger audiences. Often it is noticed that a movie is about a group of teenagers that look like they’re in their 20s and 30s as they are played by older actors. Older actors playing students usually contribute to fostering body image issues in teens as the characters meant to represent high school students are not accurately showing what a teenager looks like. They look nothing like actual teenagers, and it gives kids false expectations of others and themselves growing up. One of the biggest problems with the way high school is portrayed is that it sets an unrealistic idea of what high school should be like. Another unrealistic trait is that in teen movies and shows, you rarely if ever see them wear the same clothes twice. They don’t just wear clothes either, everything they wear is actual outfits that no teen would wear.

Even though modern teen movies have tried to diversity casts for the most part cliques and stereotypes are still prominent. High school does still have groups that students fall into but teens aren’t limited to just one social group. One of the weirdest characteristics of these tv shows and movies is that the characters never seem to study. The main motive for going to school is to receive an education. However, in the movies, the characters do everything but study. They are all either very good at sports and don’t need to study to get into a good college or they just don’t care. Unlike in real life, the characters had a lot of free time during classes which are not true in real life.
Teen movie expectations of a high school experience are almost always wrong. It is important for these mediums to at least try to make an effort to stay true to the reality of most high schoolers. Although, there are a few movies that have tried to depict high school in a more normal manner by being less dramatic and more diversified.
