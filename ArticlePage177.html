 Has FRIENDS influenced Indian Culture?


Megha Lahiri

Dec 1, 2020   |   Entertainment



Do you know of any show that took Coffee culture to a new level? A show where the actors earn 150 crores annually, 25 years after the release of the show? Today I will talk about the same legendary show in question, which as most of you have guessed correctly is none other than Friends or as some prefer it, F. R. I. E. N. D. S.

In 1993, although we had sitcoms like "Cheers and Seinfeld" in the young adult space, there was hardly any show that depicted the highs and lows of urban relationships with humour and panache . The 'Pilot' was written in 3 days, and when it was released it had 22 million viewers tuned in and was a knockout success. The biggest strength of the show was of course its casting and writing. At its peak the show was written by 14 writers. 'Friends' was so successful in the UK that according to a 2010 report, 11 million British women sported the Rachel Greene haircut. When 'Friends' premiered in India in 1999, it was an instant hit. As in the early 90s and early 2000s, Indian shows like 'Hum Panch', 'Hip Hip Hurray' and movies like 'Kuch Kuch Hota Hai' and 'Style' were unable to portray the friendship and adulting experiences with utmost sincerity. During the 90s BPOs or call centers came into being. Which led to young audiences to migrate to the cities and start living on rent with their friends. Post globalisation foreign brands started arriving in India. So just like the characters on the show, we were eating at Mcdonalds and KFC. We wore the same Levi's shirts. Many countries like India used 'Friends' to learn English. The show was also reportedly used as an example to make employees of Indian call centers emulate the American accent.

As 'Friends' grew bigger the lifestyle showcased by the show also became popular. After the release of 'Friends' there was a flood of coffee chains in the world. The first Cafe Coffee Day in India opened in 1996. After 'Friends' premiered in India in 1999 the chain expanded bold and rapid expansion.

Now the question which needs to be addressed is why is Friends an audience favourite even after 25 years? Why is the show on Netflix's top 10 list every week? This is all because 'Friends' exudes a soothing energy and the concept of family of choice. The show has examined and portrayed this very beautifully. Millennials call this show basic but that's the secret behind its success. It portrayed the thrill of adulting. Of venturing into the real world all on one's own. It explored the indispensable nature of friendships, the mess of romance and the euphoria of love and the ache of heartbreak. But it did all this with just an adequate degree of humour and pathos. It is true that in the modern context there are several problematic areas to be identified in the show and its depiction of events in general beginning with toxic romantic fixations, queer stereotyping, ethnic exlusivity and deep rooted chauvinism.
