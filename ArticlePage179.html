 Taylor Swift:Singer-Songwriter and Businesswoman on the inside


Mohan Rajagopal

Dec 1, 2020   |   Entertainment



Two months since the release of her eighth studio album, Taylor Swift is doing better than ever. Producing a #1 single on the Billboard Hot 100 with ‘Cardigan’, a #1 album on the Billboard 200 with ‘Folklore’, and joining the likes of The Beatles in the list of acts with at least 6 albums to have retained the top spot on the charts for 4 weeks (being the only woman in the list, and the only artiste to have debuted this century, no less), her songwriting skills are undeniable. ‘Folklore’ has managed to smash one record after one another, with Swift being the first ever to debut a #1 single at the same time as an album, the artist with the highest number of iTunes chart-topping singles as well as Hot 100 entries, and spending the highest number of weeks atop the Billboard Artist 100 chart. In dominating the music industry and charts to such an extent, musicality is not the only factor that is required, a fact that is epitomised by Swift. Although she may be a songwriter foremost, she could very well run a business empire of her own, and her career in the industry is a testament to this statement.

The ‘Folklore’ era isn’t the first to display Swift’s supremacy in the monetary aspect of her career. ‘1989’, ‘Reputation’ and ‘Lover’ have all been the highest-selling albums of their respective years, most notably due to the marketing technique of album bundles and deluxe editions. While ‘Lover’ offered four different deluxe versions of the album with extracts from her childhood diaries and journals, ‘Folklore’ presented a whopping total of eight editions, complete with special limited edition artwork and posters, and most importantly the bonus track ‘The Lakes’, which was exclusive to the physical albums and not released for streaming till the 18th of August, a full month after the release of the actual album. Merchandise bundles were also a prevalent tactic used, with a digital edition of the album being automatically sold along with any merchandise bought by fans on the official Taylor Swift website. The allure of an unreleased bonus track, compounded by the multiple iterations of the album and the sale of merchandise that would undoubtedly be a collector’s dream, led to more than 800k copies of the album being sold in the first week alone.

While merchandise bundling is a technique used by many artistes to drive up sales, Swift possesses one key attribute that most do not: as the Artiste of the decade, her reputation in the industry is unparalleled. Fans and casual listeners alike flock to stores and streaming services to get a chance to listen to her music as soon as possible, and the surprise release of ‘Folklore’ only accelerated this impact even further. Media outlets and the internet at large were absolutely dominated by the news of the latest album, announced a mere 18 hours before its worldwide release. Most album cycles last 3-4 months at best, from the release of the lead single to the album itself, and in the streaming age, the hype and excitement surrounding the artiste’s project usually die out by the time the cycle comes to an end. With ‘Folklore’, Swift ensured that a peak of media buzz and fan attention would be the perfect time to drop the album, with no chance of the news becoming stale. Although many dismiss a surprise release as being too risky, ‘Folklore’ has displayed the true power of a potent marketing strategy in the hands of an equally influential pop star.

In the weeks following the release of the album, attention was diverted to the lead single ‘Cardigan’, ensuring its longevity on the Hot 100 chart. Swift was quick to release an alternative ‘Cabin in Candlelight’ version of the song to keep up the streaming numbers as well. Soon after, at the end of August, she announced small pockets of the ‘Folklore’ album consisting of 5-6 songs each, which she referred to as ‘chapters’:

“In my head, the songs in Folklore fit together in different groups and ‘chapters’, based on how they fit together thematically.”

This sparked the release of ‘Escapism’, ‘Sleepless Nights’, ‘Saltbox House’ and ‘Yeah I showed up at your Party’ chapters, once again managing to drive up the streaming numbers for the album as a whole for as long as possible. Fans were quick to note that both chapters had the song ‘Exile’ in common, a collaboration with Bon Iver which is rumoured to be the next single. If that is the case, it is simply another brilliant business move, as it sets up the single for mass success, playlisting, and chart domination. The end result is an album that has managed to stay at the #1 spot of the Billboard 200 for 7 non-consecutive weeks, catapulting her to the spot of the highest-charting female artiste in history. Love her, hate her, one fact simply cannot be ignored at this point: Taylor Swift IS the music industry.
