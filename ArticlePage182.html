 The Digital Saga : My Perception


Rajkishore Mukherjee

Dec 1, 2020   |   Social Change

As an artist, I cannot hide this particular eldritch feeling which creeps within me when I think of the fact that the winsomeness of that grandiloquent platform, which we call the "stage" has been astringed down to a mere screen, a mere space within our respective domiciles.

That particular aura of the green room, the melodramatic and lengthy deliberations with our fellow and accompanying artists (which is usually everything other than music) before we hop on stage and much more importantly, seeing those alluring faces and noticing the amount of excitement one holds whenever they perform. Yes, I am speaking about the assemblage, our beautiful audience.

We miss all of that, and much more. Putting it out there, virtual concerts can never accomplish an inkling of what a proper live concert can do.

I pray that the current "reality" doesn't become a verisimilitude in the truest sense of the word because I believe that it cannot happen. Performing arts cannot, and should not go virtual - the entire quintessence of performing arts demands that the artist has to be in front of the audience and hence, I beseech every single one of you to stay at home, listen to beautiful music and god willing, artists will be back, performing for you all as soon as this time of peril gets over with! Let's just call the present ordeal to be a temporary solution.

What's the fun of a well planned Tihai and a cool Taan if we cannot see the expressions of amazement on your faces?! “The only performance that makes it, that makes it all the way is the one that achieves madness.” ― Mick Jagger
