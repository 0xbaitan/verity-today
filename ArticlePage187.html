 Will the Biden Presidency Crush the Hopes of a Free Palestine?


Priyanka Banerjee

Dec 1, 2020   |   Global Affairs

The US elections of November 2020 heralds the end of Donald Trump’s tumultuous presidency and introduces President-elect Joe Biden and Vice President-elect Kamala Harris as the new leaders of one of the most powerful countries of the world. All eyes now look expectantly upon Biden who has promised to bring about a series of changes, starting with controlling the rapidly deteriorating Covid-19 situation in the United States and reversing Trump’s extensive climate change setbacks. While most of this is good news for America and the world, many continue to wonder how Biden’s presidency will affect US-Israel relations and change the fate of the Palestinian people.

Back in October 1973, Joe Biden made his first official overseas trip to Israel as newly elected Delaware Senator. Biden has often described this meeting with the then Israeli Prime Minister Golda Meir as one of the most consequential ones of his life. This visit marked the beginning of Biden’s indefatigable support for Israel and its leaders.

The Obama-Biden administration amplified their support for Israel in the three crucial assaults on the occupied territory of Gaza in 2009, 2012 and 2014 respectively which resulted in thousands of Palestinian casualties. Consequently, the Obama administration, with Biden playing an important role in negotiations, signed a ten-year $38 billion military funding package with Israel.

The Biden-Harris presidency threatens to bring back the hawkish status quo on Israel. Both are traditional Democrats with a fundamental commitment to Israel as is blatantly visible on the Biden/Harris 2020 website. Biden lionized his part in securing the $38 billion ‘defence assistance’ to Israel, the largest military aid package in the history of the United States. He also attempted to oppose the Boycott, Divestment and Sanctions (BDS) movement.

In fact, many are of the opinion that Biden possesses a particular advantage over Trump who had, by siding with extreme right-wing Zionist causes, undermined a bipartisan pro-Israel consensus. Biden, however, has won the support of Israel’s advocates who believe him to be in the best position to suppress opposition within the Democratic Party’s ranks while bringing about a liberal and unanimous support for Israel.

While Biden has, on several occasions, expressed his objections to various Israeli policies, especially the settlement policy on the West Bank, it is unlikely that he would ever cut off military aid to Israel to force the nation to abide by international law. He may have initially opposed the moving of the United States embassy to Jerusalem, a move which declared that the United States of America recognised Jerusalem as the capital of the State of Israel and not as a divided city, but he has since proclaimed that he harbours no intention of moving it back to Tel Aviv. Although Biden stated that he would restore diplomatic ties with the Palestinian Authority support programmes that assist Israeli-Palestinian security cooperation, economic development etc., he has continued to follow in the footsteps of the Trump administration by promising to restore financial aid to the Palestinian Authority only if it terminates welfare payments to the families of Palestinian prisoners and supposed Palestinian attackers killed by Israelis.

Vice President-elect Kamala Harris, remarked that Israel meets the overall international standards of human rights. A cursory glance at Israel’s severely discriminatory laws can prove the fraudulence of Harris’ statement. Harris’ record on the State of Israel has led many to believe that she may be even more cordial than Biden as, unlike the new President-elect, Harris has yet to publicly contradict Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. Although Harris has often promised to take necessary steps to re-establish financial aid to the Palestinian people and address the current humanitarian crisis in Gaza, she refuses to identify Israel as responsible for the situation. While Trump may have been the worst international adversary of Palestine’s hopes for equality and freedom, Biden’s administration does not necessarily offer much better for Palestine. While Biden believes that Israel’s existence is essential as the only guarantor of security to the Jewish people, he chooses to remain blind to the fact that Israel continually robs its Arab and non-Jewish citizens of the most fundamental human rights, including their right to self-determination, movement, and right of return among several others. It’s unlikely that the Biden presidency will bring about a radical reversal in American foreign policy as far as the Israel-Palestine conflict is concerned. The Democratic Party’s central leaders remain tied to bipartisan support for Israel and impassivity to Palestinian lives while the Biden-Harris administration aims to strengthen the nation’s subsisting alliance to the Jewish State’s settler-colonial project. The Biden Presidency, therefore, presents several new challenges for those of us who believe Palestinians must be free.
