 The Morality of Cover Ups


Sheersha Gupta

Dec 1, 2020   |   Social Change

How do you know if your concept of terms like “morality”, “ethics”, “right” and “wrong” are the ones best suited to your own self? Perhaps your childhood experiences have a lot to contribute in this context. Or maybe the way your parents shaped you to be the perfect representation of who ‘they’ are.

A big part of growing up are the bad experiences a person has been a witness to, and some of them start only because of dishonesty. People in India have a thing that they say when their so-called lies are uncovered- “a lie which does well to some else is never a lie”. I’m sure plenty would agree with the phrase above but the real question is if we can ever truly understand the nature of lies that do good to someone? Hence, let’s talk about the art of cover-ups.

Cover-ups do not necessarily require the active manipulation of facts or circumstances. Arguably the most common form of cover-up is one of non-action. It is the conscious failure to release incriminating information by a third party. This "passive cover-up" is often justified by the motive of not wanting to embarrass the culprit or expose them to criminal prosecution or even the belief that the cover-up is justified by protecting the greater community from scandal. Yet, because of the passive cover-up, the misdeed often goes undiscovered and results in harm to others ensuing from its failure to be discovered.

Proper perspective of motives and actions is critical for a person’s natural character. It enables a person to discover themselves and other people as well, since a change in perspective will always result in you having an insight about what someone else might be thinking. You might think that whatever we derive from someone else’s actions is just a “speculation” at best, in that case how can we trust that outlook completely and ensure that there is no room for misunderstandings. We can’t ever say that for sure. But, a different view of things will always act as an incentive to the growth of our critical thinking and also reproduce ideas which are more broad-based.

The most fascinating feature about cover ups, regardless of their size, is its aftermath when people actually uncover the truth and question the point of it all. All those lies, excuses and evasions are termed “unnecessary”. But it is always the same? Can cover ups actually prove beneficial? Are they a necessary evil?

Maybe with proper structure and planning , cover ups can actually avoid a state of chaos and uneasiness. But the limit isn’t the sky for this one. They ought to stay within the limits of selflessness and for the good; never shall they ever get into play for selfish reasons. There is no right or wrong with such things. They either create or destroy. Right and wrong are claimed to be two sides of the same coin but there is literally no way in which define their stance with proper reasoning. Bringing back the point made at the very beginning, the concepts of right and wrong, black or white, game of thrones or lord of the rings- all of it starts with us: our understanding and perspective on everything. Uniqueness has always been the cause of big changes in society and to question the morality of anything in this wold is seemingly impossible on true grounds, unless you are the editor of an Indian news channel.
