 The Evolution of Rap


Dia Marik

August 1, 2020   |   Entertainment

When we think about what rap was like in the 90s, or even in the early 2000s, we think about huge names such as Kanye West (I am still so shook he is actually running for President), Snoop Dogg, Jay-Z, Notorious B.I.G, Tupac Shakur and the likes. Now what is pretty similar among a lot of these rappers is that so much of their music is just about their success and their fame. A lot of these rappers have also been drug dealers and gang members in the past. Their raps make it pretty evident that they want to portray this thuggish persona, the roots of which can be traced back to their lives in the past.



We also had rappers like Eminem who would shock us with their stunning ability to rap amazingly fast, without mumbling for a single second. 1560 words in 6 minutes and 3 seconds! Mind blowing! (And I cant even speak without saying 'uhh' after every three words, yikes). Eminem was one of the few rappers back then who wouldnt (like the others) rap about his hundred Lamborghinis. Rather, his lyrics, like his persona, were a lot more relatable. He would rap about deeper concepts that appealed to a lot of people. Eminem is probably one of the most iconic rappers out there. Selling over 220 million records worldwide, he is also one of the best-selling music artists in the world!

Not to forget some very famous female rappers from back then- Missy Elliot, Lil Kim, MC Lyte etc. Though I haven't personally heard much of their music, they undeniably have paved the way for all present female rappers like Nicki Minaj, Cardi B, Megan Thee Stallion among others, to make a name for themselves in the industry..

Today's rapping style includes mumbling and using a HELL lot of auto-tune which was not really prevalent during the 90s. I know a lot of people aren't really fond of auto-tune but in my opinion it isn't really bad. I genuinely feel, auto-tune isn't bad when a rapper knows how to take control of it and can use it like an instrument to enhance his or her vocals. And when it comes to mumble rapping... it's okay if you don't understand the lyrics of the song whilst listening to it for the first time, trust me, being a fan of today's rap, I myself can't comprehend most of the lyrics without searching them up. Not only the lyrics, but the beats and the tunes of the songs attract me. A good song to me is a song to which I can vibe to for hours, I don't necessarily need to lip sync to the lyrics, I just move with the music!

To conclude, I just want to say that rap is actually so diverse! From raps about relevant and deep topics to raps about fun and games and from slow emotional raps to fast and fiery raps about ambition, I think there's a little something for almost everyone to enjoy!
