 A Study In Mundanities


Geetanjali Roy

September 1, 2020   |   Creativity

3rd August, 2020, Monday.
I woke up earlier than I do,
And with my slightly chipped, slightly scratched coffee cup warming my palms
(Us broken people, our only favourites are other broken things)
I watched the horizon brighten with orange, and gold and bleeding pink,
And wondered if isolation has made sunrise more gorgeous–
A reminder that the earth still turns and there is still truth
Amid the surreal nature of existence now,
Even if truth simply happens to be the inevitability of time.

4th August, 2020, Tuesday.
There’s a trail of ants,
And they traipse their busy way across my study table, onto the windowsill
(They’ve been there for months, and I haven’t had the heart to kill them)
A heavy wind blows in through the opened panes, and their ordered lines scatter,
And there’s a lone ant on my arm, trying to find its way back to the table.
It bites me, and I blow it away, reminded uncomfortably of myself in the aftermath of you,
Scared and alone, lost in the wilderness
Of a world I have forgotten to navigate alone.

5th August, 2020, Wednesday.
My mother will be horrified.
There’s an ink blue stain spreading across my white bedsheets,
(Freshly changed, pristine linen, untouched by the dust of everyday existence)
From the uncapped pen I left on the bed,
Forgotten yesterday, all too prominent now.
Us artists, we’re a little like uncapped pens.
If we cannot capture you with mere words on a page,
We will bleed our fevered passions dry on your bed, on your walls, all across your heart.



6th August, 2020, Thursday.
The clock down the hall has been losing time.
It’s stopped now, stuck with its reaching hands on 9.21
(The AM, the PM, it doesn’t matter, a stuck clock is an eternal moment)
I am reminded of my grandmother.
She used to be the sharpest mind in the family, a powerhouse of dates, and times,
An unending reservoir of memory, but she too, like my broken clock
Has been losing time.
Now she asks her maid what her son’s name is, and my father looks away with a grimace.

7th August, 2020, Friday.
I don’t like my coffee bitter.
I take it without milk, with three full spoons of sugar,
(I don’t see the point of punishing myself with my beverages).
I think I have a bit of a sweet tooth, to be honest.
All my preferences point that way,
Candies that dust my fingers with white,
Coloured sugary nightmares they put at altars during worship, and I–
I used to like you.

8th August, 2020, Saturday.
Time blurs these days.
It feels like yesterday was Monday or perhaps Sunday, or July or January
(Because nothing feels real, and it’s unbelievable how it’s August already)
My mother laughs at me, calling me old and decaying.
Perhaps she’s right.
I stopped feeling young sometime between last year and this one,
And the weight of all the lives I am not living
Is racing to outweigh the burden of the life I am wasting.

9th August, 2020, Sunday.
I watched the sunset on my roof.
I watched it lying down on the unforgiving concrete
(I have to shampoo today anyway, what’s life without a little dirt?)
The sky turns gold, then orange, then pink and then the dark blue of surly clouds,
And the birds with their dark wings silhouetted against a painted sky,
Fly home. I watch, some part of me recognising the significance of the moment.
I breathe in. Breathe out. Get up, and go back home.
