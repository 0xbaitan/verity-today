 Maybe This Is The Right Thing To Do. What Do You Think?


Anayaa Agarwal

August 31, 2021   |   Mental Health


Sometime or another in our life, we all rely on Taylor Swift to help us bail out of a toxic relationship. Becoming the reprimand of every bad friendship ever was her famous lyric “band-aids don’t fix bullet holes”. Even non-romantic breakups are horrible because we go into a relationship thinking this person will be my rock and have my back. Yes, every human relationship has drama, fights, and misunderstandings but if they leave you stressed, angry or sad every time and if it’s a straight yes- opting out of the relationship is much better, as harsh as it sounds: “You are allowed to like your friends. But if you don’t like your friends… get new ones”. But only if dumping someone in a relationship was as simple as a KonMari cleanse on your social media list. They say it takes two to tango, there is no toxic person but rather, the dynamic of two people ends up becoming toxic and harmful for your mental health. Sometimes people just don’t fit together, but it’s hard to point out if it's simple human mistakes and miscommunication or a relationship that you need to distance yourself from.



A whole bunch of drama, chaos seems to follow toxic friendships: they are always arguing with someone, causing issues and unbelievable things keep happening to them. They make everything about them. According to them, everything revolves around them. They manipulate the conversation that you start so that it's back to them regardless of the topic. A toxic person will barely ever compliment you. They’ll never pick on you but never congratulate you either. You have an idea, they’ll say it’s not sensible or dismiss it; they keep telling you, whenever you tell them something, that it’s not good enough and they are likely to kick you when you’re down. It’ll feel as if you aren’t ever happy talking to them, like you can’t be yourself around them because they’ll judge you. They don’t just put you down but also act hypocritical. They won’t ever feel bad for putting you down just because they are dramatic and irrational. You just have to let them get away with what they did because you don’t want to ruin the friendship and set them off. And if it's anything like this the best advice is to distance yourself and work on yourself. A toxic relationship like this will have you be socially distanced, cause anxiety, lower your self- esteem and confidence.

“Because we are so consumed by a toxic relationship, we may have distanced ourselves from others who could have been close and kind. In order to have the strength to have a ‘break-up’ conversation, I usually recommend working towards forming more genuine, healthy bonds with other friends in order to feel supported.” - Rhea Gandhi.

In the end, when you realise that it's toxic and harming you, cutting off ties almost seems nightmarish. Because positive change is sometimes scary, holding on seems to be the better idea, but, at the end of the day, it’s not, because they affect you so much more than you realise. So, take it day by day. It starts with believing in yourself, because in a toxic relationship, the person has made you believe that what they say is the best. They dictate our perceptions of the world and of ourselves, making it match theirs, and destroy our perspectives. In a manipulative relationship, people often tend to take your truths, and stories, twist them to incorporate small lies in them and make you believe that their version is true. Believing in yourself and building up your confidence again is essential. Then move forward with setting boundaries and personal space. If they always make you live in constant fear, like you are scared to not reply to their texts or calls, remember it’s your life, they hold nothing over you and can’t control you.

“It’s okay to not be available all the time, not text back immediately or generally feel responsible for them. No healthy relationship demands that of you,” says Hingorani.

And then it goes on with suggesting a break from the friendship. It removes the idea of cutting off the ties completely. Taking time for yourself, getting breathing space and re-evaluating your choices with a fresh mind is the way to go. And, after that, if you feel any toxicity in the dyname, you will have much more strength to cut off ties. If you believe that cutting off ties with this person will lead to you being judged and they will end up hating you, remember that you will be judged one way or the other, so why not do yourself a favour and let your haters judge your success and have your positive support system have your backs and be your rock. Someday you will find the Monica to your Rachel, but right now it’s important to remove all the negativity from your life and live it the best you can, and along the way life will give you the best moments and people that you will connect to like the FRIENDs’ iconic cast or your iconic relationship, friendship duo!
