 National Minority Mental Health Awareness Month


Nysa Malhotra

August 31, 2021   |   Mental Health


A lot of individuals are aware of World Mental Health day, but not many of us who aim at spreading awareness regarding mental health issues or claim to be mental health activists know about National Minority Mental Health Awareness Month, which is sometimes just referred to as minority mental health month.



What is minority mental health month?

This is a nationwide effort which had been initiated in America in order to bring to light the mental health experiences of people belonging to the BIPOC ( Black Indigenous People Of Colour) community.



Why is it necessary for us to know about it?

Reports from confirmed sources suggest that these minority groups have effectively shown higher inclination towards PTSD, committing suicide along ,other mental health disorders etc. It is the era of acceptance for every caste, every community, every skin colour, every individual for whosoever they actually are but coexisting in this society which such rich thoughts are still the inequalities faced by the people belonging to the BIPOC community just due historic and cultural barriers. The hate they are subjected to is more than just something which is 'wrong' or ‘unethical’ & it is high time we educate not only ourselves but also the people around us in order to eradicate such feelings of 'hate’ a lot of people still harbour for them.



In 2008, the month of July had been declared as the National Minority Mental Health Awareness Month.

Some lesser known facts:

• 14% of Native American & Alaskan Native Adults had both mental as well as substance abuse disorders.
• A lot of individuals who identify as members from the BIPOC community suffer from a mental health condition per year & lack treatment due to the very fact that they are members belonging to the BIPOC community.
• Reports suggest that only one amongst every three, who belong to the BIPOC community receive mental health treatment or care.



How can we contribute?

We can contribute by spreading as much awareness as we can & doing all in our capacity, in terms of speaking more about this issue on all platforms wherein our voice can make a difference. It’s high time we do the most we can as individuals because although speaking about how mental health is important is good, highlighting that there are groups which cannot even access mental health care due to cultural barriers is truly the need of the hour.
