 Symptoms, Causes and Treatment of Teen Depression


Sayani Kar

August 31, 2021   |   Mental Health


Depression is such a taboo topic in various communities. However, diving deeper, teen or adolescent depression is overlooked even more. The main reason behind it is that adults usually view teenagers lashing out as a sign of hormonal havoc. Depression influences more than a person's disposition. It affects thinking, too. It meddles with the ability to notice or enjoy the good things in life. There are so many symptoms of depression and they need to be diagnosed in order to treat them.



Symptoms of Teen Depression

Symptoms of teen depression can be varied in nature. They may have emotional and behavioral changes. The symptoms are listed below:-

• Appearing despondent, irritable and disquiet.
• Physical changes such as sudden loss/gain in weight and appetite.
• Sudden disinclination to perform activities which were once pleasurable.
• Suicidal thoughts.
• Feelings of culpability, inconsequentiality and helplessness.
• Major changes in sleeping habits.
• Difficulty in paying attention.
• Worsening school performance.
• Withdrawal from family and friends.
• Feeling indolent to perform the basic activities.
• Experiencing ineffable pains and aches.

1. Negative feelings: Teenagers are more prone to experience negative feelings than adults. They may feel hopeless and doubt their self worth. Feelings which might seem innocuous to others might be serious for teenagers who show symptoms of depression. They also start doubting everything that they do and lose their confidence. They have private thoughts which make their lives difficult.

2. Negative thinking:Teenagers with depression get stuck in a cycle of negative thinking which, in turn, makes them focus on problems. It can make things seem bleaker than they really are and makes one pernickety. Negative thinking invades their mind and makes them believe that they won’t get through their rough phase. These thoughts might make them feel repulsive and unlovable.

Causes of Teen Depression

There can be a variety of causes behind teen depression.

1. Abuse: If teenagers are exposed to physical, mental or sexual abuse then it can be detrimental for their mental health. Teenagers may become vulnerable to depression if they are abused.

2. Brain chemistry: Neurotransmitters are naturally occurring brain chemicals that carry signals to other parts of your brain and body. When these chemicals are abnormal or impaired, the function of nerve receptors and nerve systems changes, leading to depression.

3. Early childhood trauma: Any traumatic event such as death of a closed one, bullying, etc., can lead to depression if not treated.

4. Illegal drugs: Teenagers tend to do drugs to fit in, due to peer pressure and various other reasons without knowing its innocuous repercussions. Street drugs and MDMA are known to be a cause behind the rising teenage depression.

Treatment of Teen Depression

Therapy is the tritest method to treat depression among which the frequently used methods are cognitive behavioral therapy, interpersonal therapy, and psychodynamic therapy. Medications are also involved in the treatment of depression. A combination of both therapy and medications can be extremely effective among teens who are suffering from depression. If a teenager shows lethal signs of self harm then he/she must be admitted to the hospital.

The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has sanctioned the following 2 medicines for teen depression — fluoxetine (Prozac) and escitalopram (Lexapro).

Although most antidepressants are innocuous, the FDA requires all the antidepressants to contain a warning. In certain cases teenagers even have an increase in suicidal thoughts in the first week of taking the medication. However, in most cases taking antidepressants with a doctor’s approval is highly effective among adolescents experiencing depression. Antidepressants, in the long run, reduce the risk of self-harm or suicide. However, one needs to keep in mind that everyone is disparate and it might take some time to choose the correct medication for teenagers. Medicines need to be carefully monitored considering overdose can be noxious to one. If your teen has pestering side effects, he or she shouldn't stop taking an antidepressant.

Psychotherapy

Psychotherapy, also known as psychological counseling or speech therapy, may be a general term for treating depression by talking regarding depression and connected problems with a mental state professional.

Through customary sessions of psychotherapy, teenagers can:

• Identify and try to modify their unhealthy behavior precedent.
• Explore relationships with others.
• Reprocure self worth, confidence and happiness.
• Setting realistic goals for the future.
• Assists to ease feelings of helplessness and depression.

Lifestyle and home remedies

Parents/guardians are a teenager's best advocate to help them succeed. In addition to professional treatment, here are some steps parents and teenagers can take that may help:

• Parents need to make sure that teenagers attend the appointments and consume the prescribed medications even if they are reluctant to do so. Pangs of anxiety or depression may relapse in case teenagers stop sticking to the treatment plan.

• Educating themselves on depression may not only help parents become closer to their teens but also help them identify their struggles. Teenagers should also educate themselves on various mental health topics so that they can communicate effortlessly with their closed ones.

• Having an open conversation with teens might help them feel comfortable around their parents. They will open up about their struggles with more ease.

• It is extremely important for parents to pay heed to the warning signs of depression. If teenagers are being vocal about their struggles, they should not dismiss them as mere hormonal havoc.

• Teenagers, as well as parents, should educate themselves on the deleterious repercussions of drugs. Parents should ensure that their teens learn the various harmful consequences of taking drugs and have a healthy conversation on this topic with them.

Alternative Methods

Alternative methods include the use of unconventional methods, such as complementary medicine. Complementary medicine is a unique perspective used along with conventional medicine — known as integrative medicine. The side effects of this treatment should be well versed before opting for this method.
