 Catcalling and Sexual Harassment on Social Media


Aarna Garg

August 31, 2021   |   Social Change


Is catcalling a compliment or a way of enforcing rape culture? While catcalling on streets has been reduced it does not end there, as this harassment now extends into social media. The increased presence of social media platforms in our daily lives allows catcalling and sexual harassment on social media. Catcalling is the objectification of someone, it is the act of harassing and often sexually suggestive, threatening or derisive comments at someone publicly. It can be derived from the sexist idea that if a woman dresses a certain way or posts certain images, she is seeking out male attention.



The issue is not personal, rather it’s a societal issue rather than supporting people who have faced sexual harassment and catcalling our society often suggests it was the victim’s fault and that they were “asking for it”. Firstly, nobody asks to be put in an awfully uncomfortable position and secondly, nobody wants to hear inappropriate comments about their body and be objectified. The false equivalence between how someone dresses and the obscenities they receive need to be dismantled. Catcalling is sexual harassment and is oftentimes misogynistic, it’s inexcusable behaviour whether conducted online or on the streets. It is never okay to objectify or put someone down based on their appearance or clothing style. The assumption that someone is “asking for” sexual attention is not justified regardless of gender or occupation or clothes. Appearance or clothes should never warrant unwanted obscenities from strangers especially online.

Online catcalling can give victims extreme discomfort. How can we ensure body positivity when we can’t even post a picture on social media without being catcalled or harassed? Social media is supposed to be a fun space for people to share content, they should not have to worry about inappropriate comments and being harassed. When someone is harassed or catcalled, nobody should make them feel like it’s their fault or that by wearing certain clothes or posting certain images they were “asking for it”.

Often women are told that it is a normal thing, it should not be talked about the same way people talk about the sky being blue or water being wet. People make it seem like catcalling and harassment was seemingly a rite of passage like first love, first period and exams. According to society, men are entitled to comment on our bodies and “compliment” us as they please and that it was normal to be catcalled on social media. We are often told to simply ignore the person and block them but that does not stop them from harassing the next person and making them feel insecure about their body. We need to raise our voices and put an end to catcalling on social media and streets once and for all. Other people facing these issues should know that they are not alone and the harassers should be aware of the serious consequences to their actions because now there are multiple norms on social media that protect people from harassment to make social media a safer place.
