 Pride beyond the month of June


Dipali Banka

August 31, 2021   |   Social Change


Disclaimer: Mention of Sexual Assault



The Stonewall uprising, also famous as the Stonewall riots began when on the 28th June’69 the NYU city police raided a gay bar in Greenwich Village, New York The Stonewall Inn, which enraged several riots comprising of the bar advocators and residents from the quarter playing a major role in this activism, leading to a six-day protest outside the bar on Christopher Street and also brought out people from various corners of the world in support of such a necessary revolutionary movement. The first pride walk was a riot and it also marks the anniversary of the stonewall uprising and has been one of the many traditions henceforward.

In India, the landmark event in the history of the LGBTQIA+ community was in 2016 when five people filed a writ petition in Supreme Court challenging the constitutionality of Section 377 of IPC and alleging it as a direct violation of the fundamental rights. The petition was first presented to the former CJI Justice S.A.Bobde and Justice A.K.Bhusan on 29th June’19. The case Navtej Singh Johar and other VS Union of India was heard in a five-bench seating starting from 17th Jan’18 to 20th July’18. On 6th September the court found that criminalising homosexuality would deliberately violate the fundamental right of equality therefore it was declared unconstitutional.



Often women are told that it is a normal thing, it should This brief idea of how the LGBTQIA+ community was given equal rights and liberty tells us about the long diligent measures and stances the spectrum had to take for an overdue recognition in the society, for being accepted for their sexual preferences and living a dignified life.

However, society still finds ways to build walls around them and cage their freedom of sexual orientation through the practice of gay conversion therapy and curative rape.



Right-wing religious groups believe that an individual’s sexual orientation can be ‘corrected’ through prayers or other forms of religious efforts, or through so-called gay conversion therapy. Gay conversion therapy is used to suppress the homosexuality of an individual or from living as a different gender than the one assigned at birth. Even though the practice is discredited by WHO and 50 other health professional associations but due to discrimination and societal bias against the spectrum it is practised by several others in the profession. Only six countries – Brazil, Ecuador, Germany and Malta have been found to ban the usage of such practice in a survey conducted by ILGA WORLD while another four countries- the US, Canada, Australia, and Spain have laws preventing the practice in some wings of their countries. In India, we do not have a separate law banning conversion therapy but has decriminalised it along with recognising that it is not a mental disorder that needs treatment to ‘fix it and even though the Mental Care Act of 2017 can be used to grant limited protection but it does not compensate for the need a separate law for addressing this issue. Conversion therapy harms mental health and can lead to depression, drug use and self-harm especially in the case of minors.



However, homophobic rape which is a part of conversion therapy is practised more often wherein the parents get their child raped in order to ‘fix’ their sexuality or guide them to the ‘right path of heterosexuality’. According to a report of the Crisis Invention team in Telangana in between 2010-15 there are fifteen cases of homophobic rape reported and at the same time there are many more cases but they are not reported hence the count isn’t accurate. Some personal experiences of the survivors including men stating that this was done to teach them ‘a lesson’ while for women it was to show the ‘what exactly were they missing out’ while in reality, the impact on their sexuality was absolutely zero while their health condition both physically and mentally drained to a larger extent. Homophobic rape was known to be a cure so it was named curative rape, or corrective rape, while it does nothing to influence the sexuality it leaves the survivor with physiological trauma, unwanted pregnancy, and drives them towards self-harm and suicide.



While conversion therapy and homophobic rape are tormenting coming from the family, the outside world has not been kinder to the spectrum either. According to Wikipedia, ‘Queerbaiting is a marketing technique for fiction and entertainment in which creators hint at, but then do not actually depict, same-sex romance or other LGBTQIA+ representatives. This is strictly done with the intention to gain more audience from the spectrum and attract a heterosexual ally at the same time while portraying relationships to appeal to them. Queerbaiting is very much popular amongst artists who tend to create a false sexual identity through their actions, work and statements, it is even observed in several fiction modes of entertainment such as films and television series. Many known singers have pulled a PR stunt in order to gain the spectrum’s confidence like the boy gone solo of the One Direction - Harry Styles his song ‘Medicine’ was an explicit description of a homosexual encounter, which was later labelled as “bisexual anthem” by his fans and suddenly he was the queer icon of the generation. But right after that his first single ‘lights up in 2019 he was seen dancing with barely clothed men and women, and it was found in correlation with imagery in the lyrics “I’m not ever going back” and “Lights up and they know who you are” which was released on National coming out Day, doesn’t seem like a coincident since both the songs suggested a hint of his sexual appeals as a bisexual, and yet he denied about identifying as the same in the media, which also gives strong evidence as a marketing scheme and exploitation of the queer community which he does not want to own up.



Calvin Klein featured a same-sex kiss between Bella Hadid who identifies as a heterosexual, and a robot, this was a clear queerbaiting to draw the audience and portraying a scene that is queer-friendly content, without any intention of representing queer relationships. They later addressed the same feature apologising for any offence caused. Recently in June the world-famous “bad guy” singer Billie Eilish was seen with actor Matthew Tyler Vorce, they were reported dating which has led us to question her stunt of supposedly coming out as a bisexual or lesbian in her recent Instagram post which was a behind the scene photoshoot of her recent music video ‘lost cause’ captioned “I love girls”.

Some other singers who have been condemned for queerbaiting after their lyrics contained remarks about bisexual love were Ariana Grande in 2019 and Rita Ora in 2018, although Ora came out as a bisexual later.

For someone who is still in the closet would appreciate the support of an artist who is known all over the world, their marketing schemes of queerbaiting makes it even harder for these individuals to come out and also makes it harder for them to trust someone the next time. This implication of being a queer and then denying its presence leaves a message that homosexuality is better left concealed which again makes it very difficult for the community who as it is don’t have adequate representation in media.

While we all celebrate pride month, with rainbows are wishing our friends we forget how important it is to love the spectrum for the rest of the year, because ‘LOVE IS LOVE’ for all the twelve months not just the month of June. While the pride month is to celebrate the spectrum we should not forget that the awareness campaigns and education initiatives should not be limited to a particular month, we have seen how a number of start-ups, as well as existing companies, have launched LGBTQ+ themed ads and products without donating a single cent to the charities. This representation should not be annual but must be included in the daily sales and used in a normal protocol of its business course, and as Indya Moore says “Celebrating Pride month is giving to queer not selling to queer” thus, exploiting the queer and using the rainbow as a marketing strategy should be put to an end. As an ally, we should be more empathetic and support them in their journey, as an ally we should not tolerate bullying, hate speech and threats made to our beloved members of the community, they’re as much as human as we are.
