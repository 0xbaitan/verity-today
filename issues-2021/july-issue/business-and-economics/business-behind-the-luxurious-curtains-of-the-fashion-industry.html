 The Business Behind The Luxurious Curtains Of The Fashion Industry


Shivaani Dushyanth

July 18, 2021   |   Business & Economics


Fast Fashion is the mass production of diversified clothing which are inspired by the recent trends set by famous movies, influencers or models, through designing and marketing methods. Fashion is often related to creativity, style, and self-expression, and with the development of technology, the fashion system has evolved to be more efficient and diverse. It has various luxurious brands and designers who are a part of fashion marketing.



Fashion marketing is the effective management of the supply chain which aims to maximise a company’s profitability rate across the globe. This is done by the process of segmentation and selling the goods at affordable prices, which also meet the relatability and consumer desire at the high-end markets.

However, though fashion seems to be fun and expensive, the strategies used by the companies to control the audience using their brand image is often deceptive. One of these is greenwashing, which refers to the false advertising and marketing of a company that presents misleading information about the usage of eco-friendly and green products, rather than actually implementing environment-friendly practices. This tricks people into believing that the franchise is honest and sustainable, while the company is not actually following the rules of the green movement.

Since the pandemic has had a massive impact on the fashion industry, digital shopping and demands for sustainable fashion are on the rise. So, companies have increased their greenwashing techniques to meet the consumer needs. The questionable “sustainable” H&M merch released by Billie Eilish is a prime example of such a case. However, seeing the decline of honest advertising campaigns, the pandemic has also been a blessing in disguise by testing the resilience of these companies. Business planning, crisis management and the demand by the people for non-deceptive fashion brands have increased to reduce the impact of synthetic clothing on the environment.

Rainbow-washing is another prevalent marketing tactic: a form of discrimination that aims to attract customers by displaying pride colours in company logos without actually supporting the LGBT community. Manipulation and abuse are the central themes of rainbow washing. For instance, people were extremely disappointed by the Primark Pride Collection. Primark had collaborated with Stonewall, which isn’t responsible for the organisation of marches in the UK and which refused to take part in the London Pride due to lack of diversity. Primark was criticized for donating its money to Stonewall instead of the UK Pride, which participated in l00+ local marches. In this way, Primark was accused of using gay and trans rights for its own benefit, without supporting its local pride organisations. This is also known as Pinkwashing, which makes a large number of LGBT people buy a company’s Pride products, and the money they pay holds a large value in the business world, known as the pink dollar.

The above strategic methods are developed due to the customer logistics of the company. The current design trends undergo daily analysis, solely based on the consumer’s purchases. The stores then receive this data and update their collection to reflect the demand of the people. Many of these design brands like Zara, Mango, Prada, GAP, and Armani anticipate and create new trends associated with their history and culture. In this way, every designer has a unique style of fashion which reduces the risk of confusion. This strong autonomous identity helps maintain the creativity quotient that determines the price of their clothing, accessories and makeup.

These communication tactics study a customer’s behaviour on social media as well. If, for instance, you are searching about the relationship status between the first man who appeared on the VOGUE cover, Harry Styles, and Victoria’s Secret angel Kendall Jenner, adoring Stormy’s lifestyle, gawking at Gigi Hadid’s Reebok athleisure collection or reviewing Met Gala costumes, social media marketing teams provide you with relatable content which attracts your attention to the new releases and upcoming news in the fashion industry.

In this way, every person in this world is a factor responsible for the rise in fashion. From the clothes you pick for a special occasion to the hairstyle you sport, everything revolves around the idea of aesthetics and fashion knowledge.
