 My First Impression of Anime


Dia Marik

July 18, 2021   |   Entertainment


Anime expands its fanbase every fleeting moment due to the simple fact that there’s always something new to discover, watch, or expect. Usually based on a “manga” (Japanese comic books/graphic novels) that’s already famous, an anime is created. The pre-existence of a manga helps boost the popularity of the aforementioned anime.



So, I have a lot of friends who are socially known as ‘weebs’ or simply just people who thoroughly enjoy the genre of anime. I was recently influenced by them to give anime a shot and see if it interests me. Which it did. It was rather fascinating, how I actually got attached to a genre I was never really passionate about and barely had intentions of trying, and how I ended up finishing a substantial number of series within a short duration of time. Not to mention I somehow had a lot of time on my hands.

The first anime I watched is called Jujutsu Kaisen which is based on the Jujutsu Kaisen manga by Gege Akutami. It is based on supernatural powers and dark fantasy and honestly, I don’t think the story could’ve been depicted through any other way apart from its anime form. The illustrations in the manga itself are immaculate and perfectly detailed and the anime takes it to a whole different level. The animation is so well done and leaves a heavy impact that you don’t realise when you start profusely crying because some “cartoon characters” exorcised a curse. Personally, I think there’s no way there could be a live action movie/series that could come even close to the quality of the anime because of how detailed and exact the action scenes are.

Now I think everyone has come across the concept of feeling for 2-dimensional anime characters as if they actually exist. I personally never understood how exactly one could connect their emotions with 2-dimensional cartoons. Well, that was until I watched an anime and I realised that anime characters pretty much depict real humans with feelings and personalities that are easy to relate to and for something of great gravity to happen within your character’s story arc could impose a heavy impact on someone. For someone who is sensitive towards movies/series (that include humans as actors) which have a lot of violent scenes in it, I can say that anime too has a similar effect even though it’s based on people and scenes that aren’t real. Even if it’s an anime based on fiction - the odds of any of it coming to life is out of the question. Also, the talent of the voice actors of these anime characters is probably one of the major reasons why we picture them as real entities when they’re not. The emotion and passion in their voices really makes the characters come to life, be it a simple family bonding scene or a big fight scene.

Overall, I’d say anime is a great source of entertainment as they tend to have really interesting storylines for basically every genre you can think of. The average length of an episode from an anime series is around 23 minutes and I personally wouldn’t recommend obsessively binge watching anything but trying something new couldn’t hurt.
