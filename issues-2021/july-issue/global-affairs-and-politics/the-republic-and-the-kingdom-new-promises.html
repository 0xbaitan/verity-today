  The Republic And The Kingdom: New Promises


Soham Ghosh

July 18, 2021   |   Global Affairs & Politics


Being the most recent member of this esteemed online newspaper, I was really in a dilemma while writing this article, as I have never targeted such a vast audience.



On 4th May, 2021. The Indian Prime Minister, Narendra Modi and his British counterpart, Boris Johnson held a virtual meeting. Johnson had to cancel his plans of visiting India twice this year, once in January, it was called off due to the Republic day celebrations and the second time was in the month of April which was again cancelled due to the escalation in the number of COVID-19 cases.

This virtual summit was based on the idea of elevating the India-UK ties. Both the countries launched a comprehensive ‘Roadmap 2030’ to deepen the cooperation across five key factors over the next decade.

The areas for further expansion and deepening the bilateral cooperation are based on defence and security, trade and prosperity, climatic action and healthcare. It also includes a people to people relationship. Both Narendra Modi and Boris Johnson also discussed the worsening COVID-19 situation in their respective countries. The UK has been a very essential helping hand to India in fighting the surge of infection, by providing oxygen cylinders and ventilators. The release stated, ‘‘The Summit will be an important opportunity to elevate our multifaceted strategic ties and enhance cooperation on regional and global issues of mutual interest”.



Of the nine pacts signed at the virtual summit, one was for partnership of migration and mobility. This partnership facilitates the legal movement of students and professionals, and as well as the cooperation between the two countries in combating illegal migration.

Whilst discussing the ties between the two countries, the document also consisted of the free-trade agreement between India and the UK. “Under this, the UK will open up the fisheries sector for more Indian players, facilitate more opportunities for nurses, recognize Indian seafarers’ certificates and also enter into a joint dialogue on the Social Security Agreement. In return, India has agreed to the UK's demands on fruits, medical devices, and mutual recognition of masters degrees and also works towards reciprocal opening of legal services. These actions are likely to generate 20-25,000 new direct and indirect jobs in India”, said by Sandeep Chakravarty, joint secretary in charge of Western Europe in the ministry of External Affairs. Both the countries also signed pacts regarding the deepening cooperation of Infrastructure. It was done under the new UK-India infrastructure financing and policies based on India’s ambition to deliver buoyant and imperishable Infrastructure projects in India.

India and the UK signed another document in order to strengthen the investments in both ways, this was signed in order to invest into India’s start-up sector, to give recognition to the new business ventures opened in the country, and also to support the business owners. The two countries are also ready to explore long term options for UK concessional finance in order to mobilize UK expertise in fields such as clean, green and sustainable infrastructure plans in India.



The roadmap 2030 also stated the New Education Policy of India which was declared early last year. The UK’s role as an investor in this policy will also be outlined. For Indians, the United Kingdom has always been a suitable destination for educational purposes. The package includes an estimation of about 6,500 jobs in the UK for Indians.

However, The Roadmap 2030, seems to be a very utilitarian method for the escalating relationship between both the countries, it also needs to cover certain risk factors, the project risk underpins the public/private relationship. Investment risks are unlikely ever to be totally understood and quantified. However, they may be transferred between stakeholders, better understood, and more reasonably priced, which will reduce financing costs and enable more projects to be commissioned. By meeting all the risk factors and handling it in a very discreet manner, the governments of both the countries can also look forward towards a very prepossessing relationship amongst themselves.
