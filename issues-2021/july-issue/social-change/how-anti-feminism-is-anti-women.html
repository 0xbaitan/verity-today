 How anti-feminism is anti-women


Abhishree Pandey

July 18, 2021   |   Social Change


The Google definition of feminism is - the advocacy of women's rights on the ground of the equality of the sexes. But, it has been and still is evolving. The movement started as a fight for the rights of only white, cisgender, heterosexual women, but now it includes every person from every race, caste, sex and gender. We are in an era of intersectional feminism, and it has an answer to every society-driven problem in the world. Yet we find many people shamelessly speaking out against it.



The movement fights to end patriarchy, which is one of the root causes of humanitarian issues. It helps women achieve the rights they were denied for so long, men express their emotions after being asked to deny them for ages, and creates a safe space for the LGBTQIA+ community, fighting for their rights too. It is no less than a crime to stand against something so moralistic.

Feminism has brought a change in women's lives all over the world. We would not have grown out of gender norms if it was not for feminism. Feminism strives to end huge scale issues like rape culture, toxic masculinity, gender roles, misogyny, all stemming from patriarchy. This raises the conclusion that standing against feminism, i.e. standing against the movement working so hard to bring women up to the same level as men in the society, is standing against women being able to enjoy basic human rights. Feminism fights for human rights, the same rights which should not have been denied in the first place. Feminism has always seen its goal as a society where it won't be needed so desperately, an equal society.

Sadly, we are not there yet, which is why the world sorely needs feminism. What the world also needs is for anti-feminists to realise that by doing what they do, they are not benefitting anyone, even subconsciously harming themselves. Feminism would have helped them too.

Speaking out against the movement which simply wants to promote acceptance and equality, which wants human rights for those who have been oppressed and deprived of them, showcases how the ideals of the person doing so are extremely immoral. It is something that hampers the development of society. What we need to realise is, change and growth are inevitable. There will come a time when the world is feminist, inclusive, respectful, and no amount of hatred can stop that, all it does is just make the fight a bit harder.

To summarize, the hatred against feminism is hatred against women, as standing against the movement which has freed so many women from the shackles of patriarchy and is continuing to do so, is standing against womanhood itself.
