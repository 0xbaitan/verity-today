 Juvenile crime in India


Aditi DB

July 18, 2021   |   Social Change


One of the major issues neglected in our country is - juvenile delinquency (i,e.the habitual committing of criminal acts or offences by a person who is not an adult). Young kids generally have a habit to respond in a grim and prolonged nature which often comes with a negative and aggressive perspective. Juvenile crimes are such a serious problem that even psychological, criminal and judicial experts are not able to figure out the main pattern of thinking and philosophy of the children and teens who commit these crimes. Some reasons behind these could be traumatic experiences during childhood, poverty, lack of access to basic education and moral knowledge, family violence as well as media violence. According to a study done by psychologists, most of the children involved in violence have experienced the loss of their family members.



It would not be just to completely blame these kids for what they did or are doing! Between the years 1976-1992, the number of juveniles living in poverty grew by 42% and this caused an increase in crimes in which they were involved. Also, when these children were asked when they first got to know about these acts of violence - their answers were quite common. Most of them said that they first saw criminal activities on television. Moreover, some children also added that when they saw movies they started thinking that murdering, threatening and other violence-full activities were considered to be "rowdy" and they took inspiration Of course, there is not a single doubt that heavy exposure to televised violence is one of the reasons for aggressive behaviour, crime and violence in society by juveniles.

The former Chief Justice of India, Justice V.K. Krishna Iyer exclaimed that we need a penal code because if we’re neglecting the underdevelopment in children, then we would be guilty of many faults and errors related to abandoning our children. And you wouldn't be surprised if we tell you that, in the last few decades; the crime rate by minors has only increased to a great extent. One unfortunate and disheartening fact is that children below the age group of 5-8 are now used as a mere "tool" to commit crimes.

There have been some measures taken by Indian legislation;
•India's Legislation came up with a new act which was called “The Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000.
•The Juvenile Justice, 1986 which repealed the earlier Children Act, 1960, aimed at giving effect to the guidelines contained in the Standard Minimum Rules for the Administration of Juvenile Justice adopted by the U.N. countries in November 1985.

The Juvenile Law in India at present considers age determination as of paramount significance to find out whether the offender falls under the purview of the Juvenile Justice Act or not. The consistent increase in juvenile crime in our country is a very concerning problem and it is crucial to be focused upon. The government has laid several legislations, laws and rules to reduce and stop juvenile crimes but the laws of the present and bare-minimum efforts do not make much of a difference. Therefore, the intentions are not fulfilled and the final results are not fruitful at all!
