 Social Media’s Growing Impact on Change of Social Order


Aarna Garg

July 18, 2021   |   Social Change


Over the past decade, the usage of social media has shifted from being an entertaining addition to a fully integrated part of every aspect of our daily life. Social media usage has majorly escalated the growth in the number of people who use Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat, Twitter and other social media platforms and the time spent on these social media platforms has piqued the interest and concern of policymakers and parents. We use it for every part of our lives- for entertainment, in our relationships, at work, in education and for communicating but how does it impact the change of social order?



Social media facilitates the sharing of ideas, thoughts, and information through the building of virtual networks and communities. Like there are always two sides to a coin, the same goes for social media, subsequently, different people have different opinions while some only see the negative impacts of social media they overlook the positives. Few people maintain that social media now has no practical influence, while others hold the opposite view, thinking social media is already a crucial factor in the change of social order. While the increase in time spent on social media decreases the time people interact face to face it is a tool that allows people to raise awareness and call for social change. Social media may cause health issues if used recklessly but it can be used for the benefit of society.

Social media gives people a platform to communicate, it allows people to inform others about injustices and have a voice in various social issues. These platforms are a tool in distributing information and motivating change. Social media can act as a catalyst for social change if used appropriately such as by creating campaigns for various issues, supporting causes, raising funds for charity and sharing information with one another. It is an undeniably powerful tool that has broken barriers in communication and made it easy for people to freely express themselves. Furthermore, social media has also become a tool for discussing social issues, exchanging ideas and raising awareness. There are many examples of how social media has been used as a catalyst to raise awareness for issues within communities. Social media is an essential tool to promote the change of social order because it is easily accessible to everyone worldwide, it allows individuals to share information and support one another. It instigates political change, allows sharing of information, and communicating online to promote social causes.

The Black Lives Matter movement being one example, which began with the use of the hashtag #BlackLivesMatter on social media in response to the acquittal of George Zimmerman in the shooting death of African-American 17-year-old Trayvon Martin. The power of a group of people sharing the same beliefs is one thing, but the power of millions of users worldwide standing in solidarity with one another has a great level of potential for change altogether. Change begins with how and when we interact with others and is usually inspired by social movements. Social media platforms allow people not only to be heard but also to reshape boundaries and social and cultural norms which in turn could contribute to empowerment and the change of social order which is crucial for growth and development of individuals and societies.
