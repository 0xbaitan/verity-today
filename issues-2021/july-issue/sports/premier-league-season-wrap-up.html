 Premier League Season Wrap-Up


Achintya Newatia

July 18, 2021   |   Sports


In a recent conversation with some friends online, one of them said that sports aren’t art, and it’s truly boring to sit through an entire sports match. I immediately took offence and thought to myself, “this person didn’t see the greatest premier league team of all time”. Liverpool got thrashed 7-2 by Aston Villa, a team that was celebrating not getting relegated from the Premier League. This person didn’t see Barcelona get humiliated and thrashed ‘8-2’, and this person never felt the joy of seeing their team win a trophy.

Well, a lot of fans did, and these are the teams that made them proud.



The Premier League saw Manchester City being crowned as champions yet again. However, they lost the Champions League final and didn’t make it to the FA Cup Finals either. The citizens lifted their 5th Premier League title, the 4th in 5 years. If any manager were to be given 1.2 billion USD to spend, they shall win 5 titles in 5 years. City kicked off the season with a poor 5-2 defeat at the hands of Leicester City in late September 2020, conceding poor goals and not scoring several with Sergio Aguero sidelined and Gabriel Jesus being unable to step up to the mark. But, the signing of the brilliant Ruben Dias for £60million from Benfica in the Portuguese League and another defender Nathan Ake for £40million was a game-changer. Pep Guardiola’s side made a rallying 27 game long winning streak and won the league comfortably by match week 25, officially scoring 86 points.



Manchester United finished 2nd, scoring 74 points, which was 5 more than third-place Liverpool. The Red’s had a shaky start to the season and were 15th for the first 2 months of the 2020-2021 season. Poor losses to Crystal Palace and an embarrassing 6-1 thrashing to Tottenham of all teams found Ole Gunnar Solksajer in between a rock and a hard place.

But, Bruno Fernandes and Marcus Rashford stepped up to steady the ship witnessing United find a new identity as “second-half fc”, going unbeaten away from home for the entire season and garnering the most points from losing positions. United was atop the table come January. However, poor recruitment and shoddy performances saw them hand City the top spot.

United’s star man and their Portuguese Magnifico, Bruno Fernandes also broke the record for most goals scored by a midfielder in all competitions, finding the net 28 times beating Chelsea ‘legend’ Frank Lampard for the record by one goal. 40million pound marquee summer signing Donny van de Beek, however, did not appear to play a massive part throughout the season, appearing mostly in garbage time around the end of games. The same cannot be said about free-agent arrival Edinson Cavani. The 34-year old found the net ten times in twenty appearances and provided some great moments.



Liverpool finished third in a nail-biting end to the season, with 69 points in a “remarkable” achievement by their manager, Jurgen Klopp. The team was smashed 7-2 by previous season relegation-threatened Aston Villa at the start of the season. They also saw main-man Virgil van Dijk gets injured and ruled out for the entire season after a nasty tackle by Everton goalkeeper Jordan Pickford in the Merseyside derby. Joe Gomez, Joel Matip, Diogo Jota and Jordan Henderson were also important Liverpool players who went out this season with injuries. Jurgen Klopp was forced to rely on the Liverpool academy but was greatly disappointed after seeing the defensive ineptitude of Rhys Williams, and hence played Fabinho and Henderson in defence for the majority of the season.



Chelsea 4th in a nail-biting end to the season, with 67 points after an average Premier League season performance but a remarkable Champions League performance, which saw them become The Champions of Europe in a 1-0 win to Pep Guardiola’s Manchester City.

After a transfer embargo the previous season, which saw Frank Lampard rely heavily on the youth and find stars like Mason Mount, Reece James and Fikayo Tomori, the Blues splashed the cash and signed players like FIFA Career Mode. They signed the likes of Hakim Ziyech from Ajax (he severely underperformed), for £30million, Timo Werner from Leipzig (he cannot finish saving his life), for £50million and Kai Havertz (a superb player), from Bayer Leverkusen for club-record £70million. They also signed the likes of Thiago Silva on a free transfer and goalkeeper Edouard Mendy from Nice in the winter transfer window.

Frank Lampard found his team in 9th place after spending 300 million pounds in the summer, and also found a place out of a job after being sacked by the club in late January of 2021. Former Mainz, Borussia and PSG manager, Thomas Tuchel took over and did a phenomenal defensive job and unlocked the potential of Kai Havertz and Mason Mount. He also saw incredible performances from N’golo Kante to guide him to a rough and tumble 4th place, a FA Cup Finals appearance and victory in the Champions League finale.



Leicester City found themselves out of the top 4 yet again even after spending the most time at 227 days in the top 4 until the end of the season. Brendan Rodger’s men capitulated in crunch time yet again and found themselves in the Europa League places. They did, however, win the FA Cup in an exciting final against Chelsea, which saw Belgian midfielder Youri Tielemans hit an absolute screamer of a goal from long-range after poor man-marking from Jorginho. The FA Cup celebrations with owner Aiyawatt Srivaddhanaprabha, son of deceased Leicester City chairman, Vichai Srivaddhanaprabha, was a sight to behold. It indeed was a magnificent sight to see a clubs owner and its players celebrating a trophy as jubilantly as Leicester City was.



West Ham United finished 6th in a dramatic season for the hammers which saw David Moyes’ men being threatened with relegation the previous season after finishing 16th, turn up the heat and bring some fresh air and competition into the top 10 and even top 4 of the Premier League. A fantastic comeback from the club and manager David Moyes, with standout performances from Tomas Soucek, Vladimir Coufal, Aaron Creswell, Declan Rice and late-season addition Jesse Lingard.



Tottenham Hotspurs finished 7th in an underwhelming season which saw a strong start but piss-poor performances and just embarrassing defeats throughout the end of the season. Spurs found Harry Kane and Heung-min Son carry them to the Europa League Conference places, with the former winning both the Golden Boot after netting 21 times and also the Golden Ball after assisting 14 times this season. The team also saw manager Jose Mourinho getting sacked amid the European Super League conundrum and 28-year-old ex-Tottenham player, Ryan Mason taken to the dugout.



Arsenal found a way to get into the top 10 and finish 8th and avoid all European football after being threatened with relegation in patches of the season. Ex-Arsenal “legend” Mikel Arteta showed his true colours to be just a greasy-haired, suave looking fraud of a manager whose true peak was being Pep Guardiola’s PA in Manchester City.



Newly promoted Leeds United, with Marcelo Bielsa at their helm, finished 9th, giving Arsenal and Tottenham a run for their money. Bielsa’s men played non-stop attacking football, which saw Leeds garner a reputation as a no-nonsense, attack-minded machine of a team. Patrick Bamford stepped up to the mark, as did new man Raphinha in a season in which Leeds fell victim to embarrassing defeats such as a 6-1 loss to Manchester United but also perpetrators of 5-0 hammerings to the likes of West Brom.



Everton were big spenders in the summer window signing the likes of Allan from Napoli and James Rodriguez from Real Madrid. Dominic Calvert-Lewin stepped into the role of the target man and manager Carlo Ancelotti started building something special at Everton. Unfortunately, it wasn’t to be, the team finished 10th in an underwhelming latter half of the season, which was capped off by Ancelotti getting snatched up by Real Madrid again.
