 WWDC 2021


Vedant Mohata

July 18, 2021   |   Technology


The World Wide Developers Conference (WWDC) that Apple annually hosts happens to be one of the most-watched and keenly awaited tech events of the year, with millions tuning in. 2021 was no different apart from the strict focus on the upcoming software.



Now, to speak of the crucial part. What did Apple announce at WWDC '21? Without a speck of doubt, it was the arrival of the new & robust iOS 15, bringing with it a whole raft of new features.

Apple has been synonymous with multiple new features over the years. This time they brought tons of new features to FaceTime â€” the notable one being FaceTime links which grant you the opportunity to seamlessly screen-share. Now you have the option to watch movies and shows from your favourite streaming services from FaceTime itself.

What Apple has done is that it has corrected their Zoom screen-share. As expected, developers had access to the pre-release beta directly after the keynote ended.

Apple also has announced several new features in the Operating System for IPad:

One of them being App Library, a multitasking control menu that enables you to switch between and split view and Slide Over quicker.

Another one is Quick Notes. They can be pulled in and flicked off from the corner of the screen, practically anywhere within the OS. How convenient is that for note-making on the fly!

Apple is also launching capabilities that would allow users to code apps for iPad on Swift Playgrounds. And it also requires no prior coding knowledge that makes it accessible and perfect for newcomers to learn Swift in an interactive and fun fashion.

But here is the big haul-in. That's right. The newly upgraded macOS codenamed "Monterey".

In terms of features, we didn't get everything we had anticipated. There's no customisation of the Control Centre or the Health App on the Mac. Even so, we can still look forward to a plethora of features.

Watch out for Shortcuts that are coming to macOS. And also AirPlay to Mac (both audio and video). You also now have spatial audio support from compatible sources with AirPods Pro.

Now you can have the much-needed reinvigorating Safari experience with more compact and flexible tab management (and syncing across devices). Perhaps, most impressively of all, Universal Control.

Federighi demonstrated with seemingly effortless fluidity the ability to use a MacBook's trackpad and keyboard to move across off the screen and onto the display of the iPad next to it, allowing for keyboard shortcuts and trackpad gestures on iPadOS instantly. Universal Control goes one step further, with the accommodation of drag-and-drop file sharing across multiple devices in a single action.

watchOS

We couldn't see the Battery and Hiking apps that 9to5 Mac had predicted. However, WatchOS 8 will feature a reworked Health experience in the form of a new Mindfulness app that integrates the existing Breathe app (albeit with a new UI) with a revamped Reflect experience, which suggests mindful concepts to practice.

Apple remarked that the Photos Watch Face is extremely popular among the community and users alike. In watchOS8, the new Portrait Watch Face can extract depth data from portrait shots taken on iPhone/iPad and dynamically insert the clock between foreground subjects and the background, creating a more cohesive overall look.
