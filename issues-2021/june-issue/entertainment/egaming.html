 My Experience Revisiting and Experimenting Online Games


Dia Marik

May 1, 2021   |   Entertainment


One whole year of this pandemic and now we are back to square one- schools closed, exams cancelled and a lot of us have all the time in the world to waste on whatever we want to (I however strongly recommend not doing this for obvious reasons). But these days have been reminding me of the days exactly a year ago when I could have been studying and finishing my syllabus for my board exam that was supposed to occur after school would apparently reopen in June or some month. Instead, I wasted my time experimenting games that were my entire life back in the day to see if they could have possibly filled the void in me that had been created owing to isolation. Nah, I’m just kidding, my situation was not that bad, like I’m pretty sure a lot of us were facing similar problems as I was always told by my friends and family that I was “not alone”.



Anyways, I’m positive I’m not the only one who approached video games to kill time, and before I proceed, I just want to clarify that I am NOT a gamer and I can’t play most games to save my life and I am also uneducated as to what the different games are and all that information, you know what I mean. As a kid all I would play was Club Penguin, Fantage and Moshi Monsters. They were all virtual world games with maps and avatars and shops and pets, name it and you’d find it there. You’d be able to role play there and basically do basically whatever you wanted as long as it was family friendly of course. Well unfortunately each one of these three games shut down for various reasons so I couldn’t revisit those even though I really wished to do so, but I had to play my games and sure did I compromise by trying a new game.



So, the games I tried out were Roblox and Club Penguin Rewritten. If you don’t know what Club Penguin Rewritten is, its basically a fan made version of the original Club Penguin which you could still play until January 1st 2021 if I’m not wrong, it isn’t exactly the same but it’s a pretty good substitute for the original, especially if you wish to go back to the site for the sake of nostalgia! Best part is that you don’t need to pay for membership as it is free, so I basically was able to do all that I wanted to do as a kid as I never had membership back then. It was pretty nice playing the game as most of the people I saw on the game were high school and college students visiting the website to revisit old memories, I understand why because it must’ve been a nice way to spend free time among the academic pressure for them, I’d say the same for me but I didn’t seem like I was under pressure back then (yes, I know it was quite alarming).



And now going onto Roblox- this is a game I played when I was actually under pressure and honestly, I don’t even know why I played it- none of my friends played it and I barely played it as a kid myself. But it was also quite interesting playing it as there are SO MANY games to choose from- from roleplay games, to parkour games, to tycoons, and even games where one can get free therapy(??) (I do not suggest joining free therapy games especially if you genuinely need therapy). So, you know- name it and you will find it on Roblox. For me personally, I go onto Roblox mainly for the parkour games(obbys) and in contrary to popular belief that these parkour games stress you out to the max, I think they rather helped me with my stress and I would play them during my breaks in between studies and it was well- somehow calming. I really had no clue playing these games would be of any use to me and considering that I had barely even played Roblox before I was positive that I would give up on the game within at most a month from when I started playing but I guess I was wrong and I am not mad about it either. So, if you ever feel stressed out you should try playing some parkour game- it may not be on Roblox but just try it out to see if you’re an apparent psychopath like me for finding your calm in these games.

Playing games for whatever reason is indeed very fun but obviously just like anything that provides with entertainment, you will have to moderate your time spent on it because you obviously don’t want to get addicted to playing something on a screen which you’re on almost 24/7 owing to school and basically everything being online. Let’s pray that the COVID-19 situation in India gets better so that we can start going out and start meeting people instead of having to depend on online games for our happiness which isn’t all bad- but isn’t really the most desirable. Stay safe!
