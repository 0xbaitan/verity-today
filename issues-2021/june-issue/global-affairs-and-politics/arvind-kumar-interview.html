 Interview With Cycle Soldiers' Arvind Kumar


Anikait Chakraborty

June 1, 2021   |   Global Affairs & Politics




Tell us a little about yourself and shed some light on your initiative, Cycle Soldiers and its philanthropic commitments?

My name is Arvind Kumar and I'm a postgraduate student pursuing my Masters in Public Policy from King's College London. I returned to India from the UK in February this year when the second wave of COVID-19 gripped the UK.

I started Cycle Soldiers, a voluntary essential delivery service for senior citizens living alone in Kolkata during the first lockdown in 2020. We deliver essential medicines, groceries, and food to vulnerable senior citizens and COVID-19 affected families at no extra cost. To snap the spread of the virus, the Government of India announced a complete lockdown in 2020 which was necessary but was also a cause for hardship for senior citizens who suddenly found themselves isolated and without any help. The movement of people and transport was restricted: senior citizens, being the most vulnerable to contract the virus, were asked not to step out of their homes. The lockdown also meant that their house helps would be unavailable.

I could only imagine my grandmother in such a situation and that thought pushed me to do something for all the Dadu's, Dadi's, and Thankumas of Kolkata. In 2021 once again, Kolkata is under lockdown like several other cities in India to bring the second wave of infections under control and Cycle Soldiers are on the ground once again to lend a helping hand to the administration

How did the concept of Cycle Soldiers first materialize?

I made a poster on Photoshop and uploaded it on WhatsApp and Facebook. My messages on both these social platforms speedily traveled and were shared by many. The Cycle soldiers reached different parts of the world to many relieved sons or daughters living abroad, with their old parents residing alone in Kolkata. With house helps retired to their homes, these senior citizens suddenly had none to help, for any emergency or needs. I decided to deliver essential medicines and groceries to senior citizens living alone at no extra cost and working 24x7 with my brand Cycle Soldiers.



I walked up to my local police station, Rabindra Sarovar Police Station. I met with the Officer-in-Charge, Mr. Jayanta Mukherjee. I explained to him the aim and objective of Cycle Soldiers. "I am proud of youths like you, who even in these critical times of virus exposure are venturing out to help those in need. Most in your age might enjoy this time and sit at home and relax.", said the OC. He asked me to meet with Mr. Bodhisattva Pramanik, another officer who stamped the permit and wished me luck. Mr. Pramanik assured me that the police would give all assistance when and where necessary. "If they stop you anywhere, I am only a call away," Mr. Pramanik said while handing over his contact card to me.

I started receiving calls from all over the world, from London, Dubai, Doha, USA. Callers from Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, and Kolkata too were seeking assistance. Many senior citizens had their children working in different cities and in different parts of the world, helpless and unable to join with families and parents due to the grounding of both domestic and international flights.

Who/what had motivated you to engage in this act of philanthropy?

It was the second day of lockdown, a Thursday, and I was watching the news. My grandmother called on the phone, lamenting that she could not go to the temple. My mother requested me to go and deliver her some halwa, flowers and also some medicines for my grandmother.

"Why did you have to take such a risk and come? What if the police catch you? I'm bored sitting just for two days inside the home," rued my grandmother. I realized the impact of the lockdown on senior citizens like her and tried to understand her needs. There were tears in her eyes when I asked her not to worry about me because I would have to visit her frequently to deliver her medicines and groceries during the lockdown.

The episode set me thinking. My grandmother was lucky to have someone to deliver essential items to her at her doorstep. At the same time, there were many senior citizens in Kolkata, living in isolation with nobody to help. I shared this thought with my parents. They suggested I do something about it. It set me thinking, and the concept of 'Cycle Soldiers,' was born.

What were the hurdles you faced (if any) while executing your initiative?

I am grateful to Kolkata Police, individual shop owners, and numerous other faces for offering relentless support to Cycle Soldiers. One of the hurdles I faced was before delivering my first order. Back in 2020, when the virus and its effects were still novel, venturing out felt like putting myself and my family at harm. By engaging in COVID-19-appropriate behavior and taking all necessary precautions, I protected myself and my family. After successfully delivering my first order, my confidence and the will to serve more people grew. I faced another hurdle when my cycle tire burst owing to the summer heat and due to riding long distances. It was difficult to find replacement tires for my cycle during the lockdown as online deliveries were on pause.

How many hours a day do you commit to this endeavor?

There is no fixed time for my service. I deliver essentials 24x7 on my cycle. As I receive a request, I head out to make sure the items are delivered on the same day. I have delivered medicines in the wee hours of the night too!

How do you feel about the fact that so many people have started up their initiatives to tackle the pandemic?

The involvement of so many people, civil society groups, and NGOs to extend support is highly motivating. We, the people, have once again come to the rescue for each other. From arranging hospital beds, oxygen, medicines, food, and other resources, citizens have come to each other's rescue. Social media has provided an almost universal platform to seek and arrange help. Numerous celebrities on social media amplified small services like Cycle Soldiers and others giving us greater traction and visibility, which helped our messages reach more households.


What advice would you give to those who aspire to do something similar?

We can all do our bit to participate as responsible citizens during this hour. All it takes is the first step. I would like to tell those who are aspiring to voluntarily help during the pandemic to not hesitate in taking that first step. You will ask several questions to yourself and will also be apprehensive but if you have decided that you want to enact welfare for society and drive change with your contributions, that idea will take you a long way. Let us use all our privileges to help those who lack them.
