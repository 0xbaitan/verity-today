 Children in War – Have We Crossed the Limit?


Tirna Ray

June 1, 2021   |   Mental Health


The 4th of June. Today, holds significance. Today, the UN attests to protect children’s rights worldwide. It is the International Day of Innocent Children Victims of Aggression as declared by the UN (United Nations).



How Did This Come to Be?

In an emergency special session hosted on August 19th, 1982 the rapidly rising death toll of vulnerable Palestinian and Lebanese children was discussed. This was in regards to the violence and acts of aggression being committed by Israel. This commitment was influenced by the works of “Convention on the Rights of the Child” -- which is a global agreement upon childhood and preservation of it.

The “Convention on the Rights of the Child” -- is one of the most ratified human rights treaties to exist following the revolutionary ‘Graca Machel’ report that was published in 1996. The report highlights that wars tend to disregard the basic rights of children, their right to education, right to life, right to receive humanitarian assistance, right to protection from abuse and violence, and the right to family unity. The ‘Machel’ study brought international attention towards this issue, resulting in the General Assembly enforcing the 51/77 resolution in that aimed to improve children’s rights.

The resolution, 51/77, helped the General Assembly’s attempts to guard the rights of children in conflict situations. The effects of war on children nowadays is more inhuman and vicious than before. Small children are affected by the increase in armed groups, landmines hidden in plain sight, mass-terrorism, and the aftereffects of counterterrorism measures.

The Impact

Many children have been hired as soldiers to fight in small battles and/or gain money for armed groups. Schools and hospitals are also largely targeted causing the mortality rate and unlawful confinement of children to rise beyond what it was . Many children, regardless of gender, face acts of sexual violence, inclusive of rape, which has been used by many terrorist and armed groups as a weapon of war.

To combat that, the UN affirms their pledge to protect the rights of the most vulnerable members of society, to stop them from being used as weapons of war and becoming the primary focus of aid from the UN whilst also providing them with the opportunity to have a complete childhood.

Recently, the ethnic cleansing, apartheid, and mass displacement/violence taking place in Palestine by Israel is concerning. The mass violence in the Gaza strip caused the death toll for children to rise up to 31 and two more dying in Israeli territory, bringing the mass total to 33 children overall.

These unfortunate 33 children, join the 3,000 children that have been victims of aggression caused by Israel over the last 20 years according to the records of the Palestinian Authority’s Ministry of Information.

The Impact

This 4th of June, we look towards the UN to help the children and ask them to help prevent the incessant violence that these children face. Children should no longer be used as targets, weapons of violence or be boiled down to a statistic. These children deserve their childhood. Upon the UN’s affirmation, we can only hope that this death toll rises no higher and that the UN can successfully save the children from any and all harm.
