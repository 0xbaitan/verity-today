 Children in War – Have We Crossed the Limit?


Tirna Ray

June 1, 2021   |   Mental Health


Unbeknownst to most people, 4th of June carries profound significance. It is the day the United Nations (UN) attests to protect children’s rights worldwide. It is the International Day of Innocent Children Victims of Aggression.



How Did This Come to Be?

In an emergency special session hosted on August 19th, 1982, the rising death toll of vulnerable Palestinian and Lebanese children was discussed. It was with regards to the violence and acts of aggression committed by the State of Israel. The global agreement to protect and preserve childhood: “The Convention on the Rights of the Child” had influenced this discussion and subsequent commitment.

The “Convention on the Rights of the Child” -- is one of the most ratified human rights treaties to exist following the revolutionary ‘Graca Machel’ report published in 1996. The report highlights that wars tend to disregard: fundamental rights of children, their right to education, right to life, right to receive humanitarian assistance, right to protection from abuse and violence, and the right to family unity. The ‘Machel’ study had brought international attention to this issue resulting in the General Assembly enforcing the 51/77 resolution. It had been executed with the hope to improve children’s rights.

The resolution, 51/77, helped the General Assembly’s attempts to safeguard children's rights in conflict zones. The effect of war on children these days is more inhuman and vicious than before. Young children are affected by the increase in armed groups, landmines hidden in plain sight, mass terrorism, and the aftereffects of counterterrorism measures.

The Impact

Many children have been hired as soldiers to fight in battles or gain money for armed groups. Belligerents have targeted schools and hospitals that has caused the mortality rate to rise. Moreover, the unlawful confinement of children saw a dramatic increase. Many children, regardless of gender, face acts of sexual violence which includes rape; a weapon of war used by many terrorists and armed groups.

To combat this, the UN affirms its pledge to protect the rights of the most vulnerable members of society. A mission to prevent children from being used as weapons of war and direct all UN aid into creating a safe and nurturing space for children.

Recently, the ethnic cleansing, apartheid, and mass displacement/violence in Palestine by Israel has become a matter of concern. The mass violence in the Gaza strip caused the death toll for children to rise to 31 and two more dying in Israeli territory, bringing the mass total to 33 children overall.

These unfortunate 33 children now join the 3,000 children that have been victims of aggression caused by Israel over the last 20 years, according to the records of the Palestinian Authority’s Ministry of Information.

To Conclude

This 4th of June, we look towards the UN to help children and ask them to help prevent the incessant violence that children face. Children should no longer be used as targets, weapons of violence or be boiled down to a mere statistic. These children deserve their childhood. Upon the UN’s affirmation, we can only hope that this death toll rises no higher and that the UN can successfully save children from all harm.
