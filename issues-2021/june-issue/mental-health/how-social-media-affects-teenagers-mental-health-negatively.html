 How Social Media Affects Teenagers' Mental Health Negatively


Tritee Basu

May 1, 2021   |   Mental Health


During the whole pandemic amidst social distancing and isolation,social media has been an invaluable tool for keeping in touch with friends and the wider world. I myself cannot imagine surviving this pandemic without the existence of social media. Although, it has taken a toll on many of our mental healths increasing depression, anxiety, social isolation and communication issues.



For many of us teens, social media can become very addictive. Researchers say that the reward region of the brain which is activated when we get more acknowledgement and ‘likes’ on social media platforms is particularly sensitive during the teen years, which could explain why teens are so drawn to social media. Not only are our brains vulnerable to so much time online, but because we often have difficulty self regulating our screen times even though we know too much screen time isn't the best for us. There are a number of mental health issues that develop as a result of too much time online.

• Depression: Researchers have begun to establish a link between depression and the high use of social media. While they have not actually discovered the exact cause and effect relationship between social media and depression, they have discovered that social media use can be associated with an intensification of the symptoms of depression, including a decrease in social activity and an increase in loneliness.

• Anxiety: We all feel emotionally invested in our social media accounts. We feel pressured to respond quickly to texts/comments, we feel the need to look perfect in photos and the need to have an attractive caption, all of which causes a great deal of anxiety. In fact, some studies have shown that the larger a teen's following online in platforms such as instagram the more anxious they feel about keeping up with everything and every post. Not only this but insecurity about our appearances, cyber bullying, mean comments and texts also lead to some of us having major anxiety issues.

• Sleep Deprivation: Some nights we lay wide awake scrolling through social media that we begin to lose valuable sleep time. As a result this leads to moodiness, a drop in grades and other health issues. Aside from feeling tired and irritable, sleep deprivation can lower the immune system and make it more likely for us to get sick.

• Jealousy and envy: Jealousy can wreak havoc on our brains if we dwell on what someone else has possessed or has experienced, that we have not. And because people tend to post only the positive things that they experience on media,, it can appear to the reader that other people lead more exciting lives than they themselves do. It is easier for us to get carried away and believe everyone else is happier and better off than we are, this also results in depression, loneliness, social isolation, anger and a variety of other issues.

• Communication issues: While social media is a great way to keep in touch with friends, it also is not the same as a face to face conversation you would have with someone . For instance,we cannot see a person's facial expressions or hear their tone of voice online. As a result, it is very easy for misunderstandings to occur, especially when people try to be funny or sarcastic online. Many of us spend so much time online checking statuses and likes that we forget to interact with the people right in front of us. For this reason, relationships can suffer when social media takes center stage in a person's life. For example during dinner with family we all usually tend to use our gadgets instead of talking to our family.

However, all of these issues can be solved or taken care of if we spend a little more physical time with our loved ones and cautiously handle our lives on social media. A good start can be setting screen time restrictions on yourself, even though it might be hard in the beginning, the results would amaze you. If you are facing any mental health issues due to social media, reach out to someone you can talk to, friends and family. Remember you are not alone and no one is perfect, reaching out makes everything better!
