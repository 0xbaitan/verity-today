 Internet Etiquette


Srinjoy Das

June 1, 2021   |   Mental Health


The Internet is pretty much the only source of social interaction that most of us have these days. And It’s massive. Growing up a generation but was exposed to the Internet from very early age a Majority of us have been interacting with people all around the world for almost a decade. So, you'd think etiquette on the Internet shouldn't be a problem for most people since we have been exposed to so many different types of struggles. Clearly that doesn't seem to be the case. As an embarrassingly Large number of people love to spend their whole day raging about pronouns in someone’s bio.



I’m sure you’re all familiar with the concept but for the sake of this article let’s discuss what Internet etiquette is. Etiquette is just the customary code of polite behavior in society and hence on the Internet. It mostly consists of Respecting someone’s personal choices and making sure the content you post isn't harmful to anyone. Respect, it's literally just respect. unlike what a lot of people like to believe It’s really not a new concept. Social etiquette has always been important to people so why shouldn’t it be on the Internet? In broad terms it just helps promote inclusivity Which I think we can all agree should be human.

Now specifically speaking, Internet etiquette consists of things like trigger warnings, tone indicators, pronouns, etc. These are three I will be focusing on for this article.

Trigger warnings: After certain traumatic events the brain ingrains sensory stimuli into the memory. Encountering that stimuli in a different context, causes a person to associate that trigger with their past trauma. I think it’s pretty obvious why trigger warnings should be necessary especially with everything going on in the world recently. while sharing posts or information about current events. It’s very easy to share something that could possibly have an adverse effect on someone else. Trigger warnings help to alert the person of possible triggers in the content they view.

Pronouns: I’m sure everyone reading this knows what pronouns are. If you’re still questioning why people put pronouns in their bio, it is to show solidarity and inclusivity towards people who don’t completely identify with their birth assigned gender and people who don’t conform to the gender binary. This is possibly the weirdest thing to get mad about. There are way too many people on the Internet who love to believe that they are superior to people who put pronouns in their bio and express it through anger and frustration. If you’re one of them do let us know if your skin itches from all that makeup after being publicly clowned on the Internet.

Tone indicators: Tone indicators are signifiers written at the end of certain statements to clarify the poster’s tone. This helps people Understand the context of a post and the poster’s intent. Both neurodivergent and neurotypical people benefit from this and just feel more included.

TL;DR, the past two years have already been incredibly hard for most people. The world outside already sucks enough for people on the internet to be grumpy about inclusivity and progression. Let’s just follow basic etiquette and make the internet a safer place for everyone.
