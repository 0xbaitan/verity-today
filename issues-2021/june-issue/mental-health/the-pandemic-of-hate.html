 The Pandemic of Hate


Tirna Ray, Anayaa Agarwal

June 1, 2021   |   Mental Health


Homophobia is a daily occurrence. It has become something to which multiples generations have grown accustomed. Something that no human being should normalise as a part of their daily routine.



Homophobia is the fear, a sort of discomfort towards people belonging to the LGBTQIA+ community. Homophobia, biphobia, transphobia and more, can be displayed in different forms. People could have a negative belief towards it, repugnance towards it or a prejudice. Using slurs and offensive terms towards the LGBTQIA+ community or casually uttering “that’s so gay” feed into the inferiority complex and self-esteem issues the community has been suffering from for years. Stereotyping them affects their mental health more than they show. People have to start realising that the members of the LGBTQIA+ community are much more than their labels and deserve an ordinary life without incessant hate.

Coming out of the closet requires immense courage. To be shot down immediately and be told that you are not "normal" is not something anybody should go through.

People do not realise the impact that their choice of words creates. The mental health of the person on the receiving end deteriorates due to the hate they receive. It is not only because of the internal battle with their emotions but also the struggle to face a world that would not hesitate to close the door on them.

We are constantly made aware of hate crimes towards members of the LGBTQIA+ community worldwide. Yet, we refrain from taking a stand and proving that we are allies and not enemies.

We hear more tragic news regularly. People are pushed to the edge every day, and one day they break down. The words hurt as much as people's actions. There is an unprecedented number of cases of harassment, bullying, and abuse by people who were once family, friends, and allies. The toll that it can have on the mental health of queer people is alarming. Most cases tend to go unreported. Therefore, we cannot necessarily rely on any statistic.

Research has shown 60% of students who are members of the LGBTQIA+ community face immense homophobia and discrimination throughout their school years. 43% of the students reported being sexually harassed by other peers. Only 18% can report their cases. Meanwhile, an unfortunate 33% choose to drop out of school or choose harsher options.

A multitude of studies has shown that homophobia causes a person to lose their self-confidence and self-esteem. More studies have shown that people who are a part of the LGBTQIA+ community tend to suffer from mental health issues or mental illnesses, such as depression (11-90%), anxiety (14-24%), and substance abuse (45-65%).

It is necessary to educate, inform and spread awareness about the horrible circumstances that many individuals face for simply loving another human being. As gracefully described by the great American activist, Barbara Gittings:

“Equality means more than passing laws. The struggle is really won in the hearts and minds of the community, where it really counts.”

Take pride in yourself. The members of the LGBTQIA+ community deserve the same freedom to love that everyone has. They have not committed crimes, unless spreading love and loving another has been named a felony.
