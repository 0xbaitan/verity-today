 Pinkwashing: Israel's Attempt At Being 'Progressive'


Priyanka Banerjee

May 1, 2021   |   Social Change


Pinkwashing is defined as the exploitation of LGBTQ+ rights to project a progressive image and distract attention from the negative actions of corporate organisations or political entities. In the State of Israel, this propaganda strategy is being used in an attempt to transform the public perception of the nation from a settler-colonial state practising apartheid policies that enable the oppression and occupation of Palestine to a liberal, queer-friendly and progressive democracy. It is also organized and funded by the government to make Zionism more appealing to LGBTQ+ communities over the world and project Israel as a safe haven for queer and trans people.



Pinkwashing is not simply a propaganda tool; it is a racist propaganda tool. When Israel proclaims itself as the only country in West Asia that respects queer and trans rights, it automatically implies that Arabs, Muslims and, especially, Palestinians are backward, regressive and intolerant. This makes Israel seem more ‘modern’ and ‘liberal’ than its surrounding Arab and Palestinian societies. It also makes queer and trans-Palestinians struggling with oppression and homophobia believe that they should be saved by their colonizer and that they will find freedom in Israel. The irony is, of course, that it is not even legal for Palestinians living in the Occupied Palestinian Territories — the West Bank and the Gaza Strip — to move to Israel.


Legally, the queer population of Israel does enjoy rights that are not available in neighbouring countries. These include legalization of consensual gay sex (1988), a ban on discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation in employment (1992), recognition of same-sex unions and provision of equal spousal benefits to homosexual and heterosexual couples (1994) and the right for same-sex couples to register their marriages in Israel even if the marriage took place abroad. However, these rights are limited only to a handful of Jewish Israelis. On the other hand, Palestinians, queer and non-queer alike, are deprived of the most basic human rights. Israel portrays itself as a democracy committed to human rights, but the hundreds of thousands of Palestinians living in the Occupied Palestinian Territories and all the Arab citizens of Israel who face routine discrimination in terms of their citizenship status, faith, education, employment and living conditions are not enfranchised in this so-called democracy. And the thousands of Gazan citizens who were killed during peaceful protests and the millions of Palestinians still living in exile or refugee camps tell a different story.

Despite being a so-called queer-friendly nation, the far-right government of Israel continues to ally itself with various homophobic and racist leaders of the world, such as Christians United for Israel (CUFI) leader, pastor John Hagee, who combines anti-Semitism with Islamophobia, sexism, and homophobia. The Prime Minister of Israel, Benjamin Netanyahu, has welcomed Brazil’s Jair Bolsonaro, Hungary’s Viktor Orbán and Italy’s Matteo Salvini to Israel, all three known for their far-right policies and blatantly homophobic remarks.

There is a growing awareness within the LGBTQ+ community that queer and trans liberation cannot be separated from Palestinian liberation, that anything less than equality for all is unacceptable. The idea that Israel must be defended regardless of its human rights abuses or racist violence for its ‘progressive’ policies alone separates LGBTQ+ liberation from the larger humanitarian philosophy that all human beings are deserving of basic human rights. Being queer or trans does not exempt Palestinians living in Israel from being harassed, tortured, displaced, bombed, and incarcerated. The systematic destruction of economic stability and opportunity within the Occupied Palestinian Territories forces Palestinians to leave their homes and live as undocumented, precarious workers in Israel proper, where they receive no protection against harassment, intimidation and discrimination and where finding safe housing and steady employment is nearly impossible. Therefore, queer or trans-Palestinians are left with only two options — either living as stateless, undocumented migrants or braving the constant violence and indignity of living in the Occupied Palestinian Territories. That is Israel’s LGBTQ+ liberation.

Oppression is a multi-faceted phenomenon; it operates through the simultaneous mobilisations of race, gender, sexuality, and class. Failing to address the intersections of race, gender, sexuality and class in the Israel-Palestine conflict creates a system of ideological cover that shields the State of Israel and the Israel Defence Forces (IDF) from being accountable for their crimes. Pinkwashing only makes sense as a political strategy in a state where Islamophobia and Arabophobia are very deep-rooted and inherent because using queer and trans issues to conceal discrimination can only be conceived as a propaganda strategy if discrimination is indeed being systematically perpetuated.
