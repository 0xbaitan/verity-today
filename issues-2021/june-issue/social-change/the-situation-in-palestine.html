 The Situation In Palestine - A Call To Help


Shazia

June 1, 2021   |   Social Change


The word Palestine roots from the Greek word Philistia, dating from the descriptions of the region by the ancient Greek writers in the 12th century B.C. Palestine was commonly used to refer to the geographical region between the Mediterranean Sea and the Jordan River. Since the early twentieth century, Arabs who live in this territory have been referred to as Palestinians. Much of this land is now considered present-day Israel.



Take a look at the chart given above. We have been made aware of the recent, rather dire developments between Israel and Palestine which has compromised thousands of Palestinian lives disproportionately. Palestine is burning. The soil is stained with the blood of its age-old inhabitants. They need our help now, more than ever. The chart above also includes the lives of children who have found themselves caught in the crossfire of dirty politics and fallen prey to it. “I put the children in our room today so that if we die, we die together and no one has to mourn the death of one another”- a paraphrased palestinian civillian’s tweets. Palestine has seen a lot of military disturbances, protests, deaths and bloodshed over the years, especially the Gaza strip - the border between modern-day Israel and Egypt - with the casualties skyrocketing periodically. Where on the first night of Ramadan we see fairy lights and decorations, a festive aura in the air. We also see corpses, smoke, the debris and rubble of partially collapsed buildings on the last day. The doctors are struggling to tend to the burns, shrapnels, bullets, chemical poisoning injuries along with the several amputations in the already financially impeded place.


Furthermore, airstrikes have targeted media buildings and hospitals to break the legs of the medical infrastructure and the press. Over 672 military targets have been destroyed by the Israeli army in Gaza. Civilian casualties continue in thousands conflated with the casualties from the skirmishes between the police and the civilians regularly. Historians have constantly maintained a very neutral narrative by calling the conflict and its roots “complicated” and several people taking inspiration from it have remained silent about the events under the excuse of ignorance. What people seem to keep forgetting is while they debate the causes of the conflict and participate in the online blame games, the deaths continue to occur; Palestinians majorly and Israelis marginally. The conflicts show no perceptible signs of abating and will, in all probability, continue till there is global interference by the superpowers which have aided the creation of this catastrophet.

What can we do as mere citizens to help? Here is a list of things you can do to aid them while those who can prevent the conflict from continuing, hopefully, do something.

1) Medical Aid for Palestine (MAP) - MAP is a registered charity and are doing active groundwork to support those in need. They are supplying trained medics, tending to wounds, dressings, fluid requirements and also supporting the central blood bank. To donate, visit their website at Donate today - help us provide urgent supplies to Palestinians in need (map.org.uk).

2) Muslim Relief - Registered charity Muslim relief have set up a page to receive donations regarding the same cause but centred around providing essentials. They aim to supply food packets, meals, potable water and clothing. Donate to their page at Kxng Sameei is fundraising for Muslim Relief (justgiving.com). You may also start a fundraiser by yourself if you like.

3) Protest - Just because you do not control the authority to step in does not mean you cannot protest about it and try to compel authorities to take steps. Successful protests have taken place in London with more than 8000 people turning up and more protests have been planned. You may participate in the same. Every count matters. Like Arundhuti Roy said “Can the hungry go on a hunger strike? Non-violence is a piece of theatre. You need an audience. What can you do when you have no audience? People have the right to resist annihilation”, the concept of peaceful protests is a paradox, the coinage itself being an oxymoron. If protests become peaceful, the point and the power of protests are lost. Just like the plagued cannot be asked to participate in a conspiracy to leak a virus, protests are meant to be assertive. Everything else is a publicity stunt meant to elicit sympathy from the dormant masses.

4) Social Media - There will inevitably be several people linking fundraisers to help Palestinians in specific numbers or as a whole. Keep track, share, amplify and donate if you can. You can find a thread including currently active donations going on by searching "donation link thread for Palestinians in need;" / Twitter on Twitter.
