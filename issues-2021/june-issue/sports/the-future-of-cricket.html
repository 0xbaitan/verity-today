 The Future of Cricket


Gaurav Rampuria & Achintya Newatia

June 1, 2021   |   Sports


As if COVID 19 hadn’t brought enough misfortunes already, up came the indefinite suspension of IPL 2021.



The BCCI had indefinitely suspended IPL 2021 on 4th May, 2021, following the rise in COVID cases in the Bio Bubbles. Varun Chakravarthy and Sandeep Warrier of the Kolkata Knight Riders were the first positive cases reported, followed by Amiti Mishra of Delhi Capitals and Wriddhiman Saha of the SRH. The BCCI ensured that all participants of IPL 2021 had reached home safely.

The IPL is the marquee T20 event each year. With the number of stakeholders involved, cancellation of the IPL is a huge deal and an immense challenge that the BCCI will have to overcome.

The bigger question to be asked now is regarding the future of the IPL. With a jam-packed Indian calendar –

• WTC Final from 18 – 22 June
• 5 Test matches in England from 1st August to 14th September
• A second-team touring Sri Lanka for White ball games
• The T20 World Cup in November.

It doesn’t even include the mandatory 14-day quarantine for players who shall return from England.

BCCI thus faces the following problems:

1. When to hold the remainder of IPL 2021?
2. Where should IPL 2021 be held? What are the subsequent arrangements to make?
3. How to ensure that foreign players from other countries can participate in the rescheduled IPL?

BCCI had recently requested the ECB to reschedule the 5th test between England and India at Old Trafford before the 1st test. However, the ECB had denied such a request, considering the immense logistical challenges involved.

The BCCI has hinted that no IPL 2021 can result in a loss of up to 2500 crores. The BCCI has been desperately trying to find a window to fit IPL 2021.

The BCCI could consider holding IPL in England in the window after the WTC final and before the English tests. In a way such that the test regulars don’t have to go through the 14-day quarantine.

There is, however, another possibility. IPL could be held in UAE either before or after the T20 World Cup (whose future and chances of being held in India are also in jeopardy!).

The IPL is a treat for not only Indians but all over the globe; it’s the toughest T20 league for a reason! But with more questions than answers, the BCCI will have to brainstorm something as soon as possible.
