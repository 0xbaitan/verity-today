 Aftertaste


Aayudh Pramanik

May 1, 2021   |   Creativity




Did you know poets fight,
With themselves about whether,
To be a poet or just, a storyteller,
Who won't always weave sentences,
Out of the way you'd butter your toast,
And the way your coffee smelled,
It's probably the same really,
Just like the way mine smells,
But it still isn't,
And it's a little bizzare,
Digging meaning out from,
Somewhere there is none,
But I promise you,
That you would not be reading this,
If we didn't give meaning to something,
And talk about it like an accidental observation
We chanced upon.

I think I left the kettle on,
It's so, so loud,
If you were here you'd scream at me
And it's the pervasive absence of that,
Which makes me want to hold on to the
Shrieking of the kettle.

Did I ever tell you that it was you,
Who is the reason behind the tales,
I recount to the neighbour's kid,
And everything I learnt from them,
That it was you who made me realise,
That time is just a social construct,
To put things into perspective.
But I like to think that there's more
To the inherent quality of development,
Than time.

I had known you for not more than two days,
And yet you make me write you letters,
Which only ever reach an incorporeal,
Extended,
Decorated,
Eviscerated version of you in my head.
Maybe even my heart, but I won't ever admit it.

It would obviously be cliché to say this,
But the knowledge of that fact,
Would only ever aid me in structuring,
This certain feeling better,
Possibly even solidify and materialise it's apparency 
But it's safe to say that maybe,
Just maybe you're that missing piece
That I needed to complete this
Soporific puzzle of a mantel piece
That I am.
Maybe you were what I needed to give purpose,
To my unreleased songs.

Now besides all of those musings devoid of a destination,
You might be wondering what this is.
It's probably just the weed,
But maybe this is just another wake up call,
To convince myself that I'm knee deep in love,
But like I've said, I won't ever admit it.
