 Police Brutality and Systemic Racism - The Need for Action


Aayudh Pramanik

May 1, 2021   |   Social Change


Breonna Taylor, George Floyd, Tamir Rice, Daunte Wright, Botham Jean, Ramarley Graham - do these names sound familiar to you? They should since they are all united by two common factors. They are all black and they were all murdered in cold blood by the same people we put trust in to prevent murders.



Statistics clearly show that Black and Hispanic people are 2.5 times more likely to be killed by the police as compared to white people. We’re all acquainted with the unfortunate story of George Floyd. He was suffocated to death by Derek Chauvin- a white police officer, who had knelt on his neck for a continuous period of eight minutes and forty-six seconds. He was unarmed, cooperative and had pleaded to be released multiple times because he couldn’t breathe. When he had been approached by officer Lane, another white police officer, he had had a gun drawn on him, the reason for which was never provided by those responsible. “If it had been a white person charged with counterfeiting, they would casually laugh about it later on, sitting at a barbeque party among friends.”, a Twitter user said - I think that is quite true, can you recall if we have ever heard the news of a white man being killed for being cooperative and unarmed during an arrest made under the suspicions of having used a counterfeit 20$ bill?

Breonna Taylor, a 26-year-old black medical worker was shot to death in her Louisville Apartment on the night of March 13, 2020. The officers involved claimed to have announced themselves clearly as the police, whereas, on the other hand, Kenneth Walker, Breonna’s boyfriend who was present along with her in the apartment, disputed the claims, saying he had never heard them say anything of the sort and as they both woke up to loud banging on their door, he had fired a shot from his licensed handgun hitting one of the officers in the knee due to a justified suspicion of being under threat from Breonna’s ex-boyfriend who may have broken in. The police responded with 32 shots, in exchange for the one, six of which hit Breonna instantly killing her. Not a single officer had used body cameras hence impeding the public from determining what exactly had happened. One of the officers had been charged with ‘wanton endangerment’ for having fired so blindly that a few of them landed in the neighbour’s apartment, while the other two officers were not charged at all. Not a single one of them was explicitly charged with Ms Taylor’s death.

Ramarley Graham, a mere teenager of 18 had been killed by the police in a similar setting. During an NYPD narcotics squad surveillance of a Bodega near his home, Graham had been allegedly seen adjusting the butt of what seemed to be a gun on his waistband and upon being approached, had allegedly run into the home of his grandmother, Patricia Hartley. Surveillance footage from just outside the front door of the building proved otherwise as it showed Graham approach the building at a walking pace, manifesting no signs of urgency whatsoever. Upon gaining forceful entry into the building, an officer had run up to Hartley’s apartment, knocked down the door without a search warrant or announcing that he was a police officer and discovered Graham attempting to flush a bag of marijuana down the toilet. He was shot square in the chest, without warning, with his younger brother and grandmother as the audience. Upon further investigation, he was found very clearly unarmed.

On 22 November 2014, when a police car was passing by the snowy field at Cleveland Park, Tamir was throwing snowballs with a toy pellet gun. Officer Timothy Loehmann shot and murdered the 12-year-old within two seconds of leaving his team car. Let me rephrase that for you - “A 12-year-old black kid was killed by a white police officer for playing with a toy pellet gun, completely threat-less and unsuspecting.”

Daunte Wright, a 20-year-old black man was killed by Minnesota police officer Kim Potter. She was supposed to have tased him. Surveillance footage of the incident clearly shows her screaming the word “taser” thrice as per protocol before tasing someone. Why did Wright die then, it was just a taser right? Turns out, Kim Potter had ended up firing her service Glock 9mm pistol instead of her taser. She later said in her defence, that she had mistaken her pistol for her taser and only realised what she had done after the ‘accidental’ discharge. The bullet hit Wright at very close range in the chest area. A few things to note here. All officers are required to wear their firearms on their dominant side and their tasers on the opposite side, to deter such fatal mix-ups from taking place. Moreover, a Glock is significantly heavier than a taser and even someone who has not trained with the weapons for as long as a police officer does would easily be able to tell the difference in weight. Tasers are also coloured very differently, mostly neon or yellow so that officers can clearly distinguish between their taser and service pistol which is a metallic grey or black. The bodycam video released by the press for public eyes shows Kim Potter, the culpable officer, holding the gun in front of herself, pointing it at Wright quite a while, long enough to be able to tell that it was her service pistol and not her taser given that she was looking right at it.

All of the evidence that I put forward, points to an overwhelming amount of apathy towards the victims, a blatant disregard for standard protocol, instances of misinformation in the reports of the incidents in order to cover up ‘slip ups’ and almost implied intentions of termination. It makes you wonder whether they were actual accidents in situations of ‘high pressure’ or instincts stemming from internalised racism leading to the taking of deliberate decisions. The statistics outline and also prove that in the amount of civilian and criminal deaths caused per annum due to police brutality, the number of deaths of black people outweighs the number of deaths of the racially oppressive races. There is only one thing that we can attribute those statistics to and that, very obviously, is systemic racism. “We have enough evidence that tells us that action needs to be taken,” says Justin Nix, a criminologist at the University of Nebraska Omaha. “One thousand deaths a year does not have to be normal.”
