A Paranormal Experience?

    • Mental Health   31/12/2021   Vaishali Sarah Mundle

    I am a sort of a person who for some reason, has always had some complication or the other with just having something as simple as sound, uninterrupted sleep. There’s this ongoing joke among my friends about how my sleep is like streaming music on Spotify for free. Be it a nightmare, an odd dream which was not particularly unpleasant, or just the uncomfortable temperature of the room, there has always been a factor leading to my sleep deprivation, which in turn started affecting my mood during the day. Now, behold, the big however, when I was thirteen, I had an odd experience. Actually, a very scary one. I distinctly remember, having a bad dream and then suddenly waking up. I was not entirely conscious though, in fact, I had to process for a good sixty seconds about whether I was awake or asleep. After finally having processed the fact that I was awake, I attempted to turn over. Now here’s the shocking part, I could not move a muscle, however much I tried. The best way to describe it would be, resting your palm on a table, folding your middle finger in and trying to lift your ring finger without disturbing the other fingers. I was inevitably terrified out of my wits and felt a sinking feeling in my chest, so I attempted to scream for help. Complete failure. I lost my voice, I couldn’t even whisper. I stayed still for the next couple of minutes and was about to cry. I managed to move my neck and finally, I was able to regain the strength to move my body parts again. This is not where it ends, I was too terrified to fall back asleep again and thirteen year old me made the rookie mistake of looking up symptoms.

    “Sleep paralysis”… okay, fair enough. I scrolled down a little further and there it was, the three words that still haunt me to this day

    Sleep Paralysis Demon.

    Now, what on this planet earth is a Sleep Paralysis Demon?

    Before science could explain this phenomenon, sleep paralysis was considered to be a demonic possession, since it feels like a heavy force is pinning you down, or the “demon” is stepping on you. Along with all of this, there plays a major factor in people to this day believing there is an actual demon. In many cases, people have seen a silhouette in front of them, shuffling towards them. I personally, in my numerous episodes have experienced that as well, and I can say this much- it feels like you’re running out of breath in addition to the loss of voice and mobility. So the best thing one can do, or what has helped me the most, is to just shut my eyes tightly. To make things a little less scary, here are a few scientific reasons for why this may occur:
    • Sleep deprivation. This is one of the main reasons for this occurrence, but it’s different for each individual.
    • Chronic sleeping disorders like narcolepsy.
    • Anxiety disorder
    • Post traumatic stress disorder
    • Hypertension
    • Clinical depression
    • Family history of sleep paralysis

    But what causes the illusion of seeing a figure shuffling towards you? Is it paranormal? Not quite!

    The answer is simple- sleep paralysis occurs when you are having your REM (rapid eye movement) cycle. This is when you have the most intense dreams since your brain is comparatively active and your sleep is not that deep, hence what you see feels life-like but is actually, all just in your mind, it’s like a dream. So to put it in simple terms, your brain is basically confused about whether or not you’re asleep and it temporarily stops your movement to prevent you from acting out physically to fight the figure you see in your dream, because I can already imagine, that would be a complete disaster it it weren’t for your temporarily paralyzed body. Even though there is so much information available on this subject recently, in spite of that, people are frightened to experience such an occurrence, no matter how many times they’ve had it in the past. Each episode feels like the first one which leads to severe anxiety. The cultural beliefs surrounding this topic do not make it any better and spew more fear. Yes, you are saved from the physiological damage when it comes to having your body temporarily paralyzed in the middle of the night, however, the long term psychological impact is immensely adverse. If you experience this at all, let alone frequently, approach someone. A parent, a friend and guardian, a relative, whoever. Know that you are not alone and do not hesitate to ask for professional help as well.

    Sleep paralysis, as of now, has no cure, so the best you can do is be aware of what you are experiencing and not feed yourself or others with incorrect information. It will only frighten you more. So here’s what I do after almost four years of experiencing it now. Research, use logic and talk about it. Will it decrease the number of episodes? No, but at least you’ll know what’s happening and not think it is something so absurd that you’d be considered weird for experiencing it and for heaven’s sake, not believe an actual demon is using your body as a dance floor!
