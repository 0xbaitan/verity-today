More of you

    • Mental Health   31/11/2021   Aarna Garg

    Self-love is simply having a high regard for your own well-being and happiness, prioritizing your needs and not sacrificing your well-being for any reason. Everyone has a different definition of self-love because everyone has different perspectives and ideas of self-care and compassion. It could mean putting yourself first, giving yourself a break from judgement, forgiving yourself, being kind and compassionate to yourself, or practising acts of self-care.

    Two major aspects of self-compassion include our inner voice and guilt.

    Our levels of confidence, self-compassion, self-worth are all determined by an inner voice- a judge that sits in our minds. It watches what we do, studies how we perform, examines our behaviour and relationships, tracks our successes and failures then eventually, it passes a verdict, which constructs our entire sense of self. The inner voice can either lend us an appreciative and generous view of ourselves or it could encourage us to be highly critical, judgemental, and often disappointed with ourselves. We don’t realise that the inner voice has always been an outer voice that we imperceptibly made our own. Criticism and judgment from others get lodged in our ways of thinking for better or for worse. Improving how we perceive ourselves, our levels of self-compassion and confidence is greatly dependent on how we perceive ourselves. It may be tempting to say we shouldn’t judge ourselves, simply accept who we are and love ourselves no matter what. However, it’s not that we should stop judging ourselves, rather that we should learn to be better judges of our behaviour allowing us to grow and improve.

    The people we surround ourselves with is a key component of our self-perception, it shapes our environment. Surrounding ourselves with people who try to lift us up, and create a healthy and safe environment is essential. At times some people make us feel guilty for putting ourselves first, which makes us feel selfish, this is a toxic environment that negatively impacts an individual's well being.

    “Selfcare is a priority and necessity, not a luxury”, believing that self-care isn’t selfish is easier said than done when people are constantly burdening us with guilt. We feel guilty for prioritizing ourselves because we think it’s selfish. Selfless people are appreciated more and are admired by many. We often feel that by giving to others, prioritizing them constantly we’ll be better liked or accepted. The guilt lingering in your head often makes it hard for you to focus on yourself, the opinions of other people become our priority and at times their negative comments lower our self-esteem and confidence.

    But why? Why do we feel guilty for putting ourselves first, for putting our needs first? It is a necessity to appreciate ourselves and care more about ourselves as it helps boost confidence, leads to more joy, happiness and satisfaction which can help us prosper and grow. Loving ourselves, accepting ourselves, working on ourselves, along with constructive criticism can all greatly contribute to an individual's wellbeing. However, toxic environments where we’re burdened by guilt can lower our self-esteem and make us trivialize this. If you can’t love yourself you can’t love others either like the old saying goes: you can’t pour from an empty cup.

    Self-love is self-acceptance that does not in any way preclude or reduce your love or compassion for others so it is definitely not the same as its malignant form, narcissism. Loving yourself is the prerequisite for loving others and for others to love us. We all are perfectly imperfect and we don’t have to dilute or change for anyone else or any reason- simply love and accept ourselves the way we are because everyone is beautiful in their own way. Just remember beauty comes from within and keep telling yourself you are beautiful because you are and the world should love you for who you are, believe in yourself and keep smiling, be more of you and less of them. Do not let anyone’s opinion bring you down or make you feel unworthy.
