Remember Her Courage

    • Social Change   31/11/2021   Vidushi Shukla

    She clenched her fists. Embraced her present and changed her future. That's what it took to crush a part of endless years of patriarchy. Ironically, I don't read about her fierceness in my textbooks; and I wonder why?

    "whispers are loud enough to be heard by the ones who want to hear", Mrs Lini said; I remember it because it has carved my memory. Listening to her anecdotes of women from the earlier period, I was moved in the manner in which I could equate with a narrative so different from my own life experience.

    Under the shining stars of the midnight in the late seventeen hundreds, as America persisted for its' freedom, Abigail Adams wrote to her husband, "Remember the Ladies, and be more generous and favourable to them than your ancestors." Years later, we're finally able to exercise primary rights- voting, driving, working in our professions and even getting an education! Their forgotten lives hold many lessons for present-day women. How did they defy the traditional belief that women were unfit for education?

    How did they persuade educational institutes to open their doors? What did they do to flee the suffocating restraints of family, caste and society?

    Marilyn Monroe: Marilyn is a remarkably celebrated modern-day feminist icon. Affirming her feminine strength, Monroe ambitiously conquered a way out of her constraints. Not once did she fail to promote herself and boldly make her mark, even after almost seventy years after her death, the marvellous star was back to invigorate me. She was not afraid to talk about the sexual abuse she suffered during her childhood and adulthood–a heavily censored subject at the time, setting a bold, feminist example for women out there!

    Anne Boelyn: During the first initial wave of feminism, Anne Boelyn was committed to the betterment of women in various fields of literature. The concept of equality between the various genders was very much a subject of debate at the time- almost five centuries ago- Boelyn was actively engaged in cultural debates regarding female education. She was an advocate for literature, guarding through the writings of modern writings by women. Anne often argued about the contribution of women in the various fields, evidencing several praiseworthy contributions to civilization, and therefore should not be excluded from universities, government, politics, employment and owning property.

    Anandibai Joshi: In the late eighteen hundreds, a diminutive eighteen-year-old girl stood up before an entirely male audience, seeking approval for an unprecedented plan. Her presence by itself was worthy of notice. The first female doctor of India, she stood firm, keeping unwavering faith in herself as she challenged the oppressive society. Out of everything she has created for girls my age, formulating a massive career path whilst battling colonialism and defamation- she also taught us that faltering has never been equal to failing.

    Aisha Taymur: A rather underrated author in the modern world, Aisha Taymur was a social activist and an advocate for feminism. She raised her voice against the deprivation of rights women had had to suffer through; Her stories encouraged young girls to 'keep their head high even through their uncountable self-doubts'. Rediscovering the literature of the ancient world had inspired the humanist learning of women's rights. Taymur spoke up for the education of women and defended them from inequality, she was of vital importance in shaping politics and culture and helped to propagate new ideas.

    As we evolve together, as a society- we must keep in mind to remember the ladies, all of whom had massive contributions in shaping the world that we live in today, making it better. And as we share our gratitude to them, we must also extend our gratitude to the ones who went unnoticed because our bravest moments can sometimes be invisible.
