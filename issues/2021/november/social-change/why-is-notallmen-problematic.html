#NotAllMen: Why is it Problematic?

    • Social Change   31/11/2021   Dipali Banka

    Let’s take a classic example of how discussions surrounding #notallmen unfold usually-

    Individual- I’ve been followed, assaulted and screamed at in the streets. My boyfriend forced himself on me; I was assaulted by a relative and groped in public transport.

    Man- Most men I know would never indulge in such monstrous activities. Not all men are like this- you can’t generalise. You never have to worry around me, I’m a nice guy.

    Despite men’s confident argument to not generalise and perceive them as a threat, their counterparts’ experiences tell a very different tale.

    When I ask my friends, and acquaintances – “What’s your first thought after you’ve encountered a man”. The responses are usually“I hope he wasn’t sexualising my body”, or “I’d keep my distance, just in case.”

    It is crucial to understand why the phrase is a trigger to anyone who’s a survivor of sexual violence and harassment. When you say NOTALLMEN, you dismiss the dreadful lived experiences of both binary and non-binary victims and serves as an indicator of ignorance of the prevalent gender privilege you possess. Survivors who have undertaken immense emotional labour and pain while revisiting their trauma, speaking up courageously on the #MeToo platform, and expect that men amplify women’s basic right to safety.

    You say NOTALLMEN but are blind to several examples of the problem in the real world. Young boys in high school slut-shame individuals, men of the working class cannot keep their hands and eyes to themselves, and somehow forget how to balance their bodies in public transport, educated male employees cannot digest a woman getting a sought after position due to merit and resort to spreading false rumours of her sexual relations with a man to get the job, hundreds of comments can be found under a female actor’s post on social media talking about them inappropriately, transwomen being harassed and non-binary individuals being trashed on a daily basis etc. And of course, the hoards of usual unsolicited nude images sent.

    We know that not all men are responsible and that especially because you keep mentioning “I am not like this”, “my brothers and I are very much well behaved” and “I respect my partner”. We know now that you would never do that because of your own constant reminders and ignorance to our experiences and grievances. And yes, it is too much to ask us to hand out badges of honour ‘thank you for not raping me or anyone’ and celebrating ‘this man is not a rapist or abuser’ because that is the bare minimum. If you dont fit into the category of men we’re referring to, good for you but you’re part of a slim minority- and we’re addressing a much larger issue. Our agenda here is not to offend you but as an invitation to make this planet a better place for us women, non-binary folks and members of the LGBTQIA+ community that suffer.

    When you say NOTALLMEN, it is a defensive technique to say “I am not the problem”, and absorbs the greatest amount of accountability, and you have to understand that this is not an issue you have to make about yourself- its a fight against violence executed by men. Saying NOTALLMEN makes you part of the problem because you are solely interested in protecting your fragile ego over a large majority’s suffering. Saying NOTALLMEN does not contribute towards anything, let alone a solution.

    I deserve to live in an environment in a society where men can maintain boundaries and understand consent, keep their hands to themselves, and I will not praise their acts of bravery for giving me the bare minimum. I will not praise you for not saying NOTALLMEN and hijacking conversations pertaining to discrimination and gender equality.

    To conclude with an example and leave you with a thought worth your time, let’s talk about three cupcakes. If you are told that one of them is poisoned and not told which one, will you take the chance of choosing one of them and risk your life because not all are harmful or would you avoid all three of them altogether because you cannot possibly identify which isn’t.

    Think about it.
