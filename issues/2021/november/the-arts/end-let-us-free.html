End, let us free

    • The Arts   31/11/2021   Niharika Rajsaha

    Your Hell is not empty and yet the devils are there. Your home is a breathing pulse, beating its way into their jokes and nightmares. A place where the dead “drink dirt and eat stone” and as the seat of ultraviolet flood lights and satyrs dancing to Twin Temple, the concept of heaven and hell through the years has been warped because as humans, probability and assumption is how they access the unknowable. They try to stand at the door between what they see and what they think they would be able to see on another plane. But they stand at the door. Like visitors coming to see the athlete who was crippled in the accident. Cautiously. Because they are terrified of breaking the image, of provoking your wrath. More importantly, of seeing a side they would have rather not seen, in case it changes the well-articulated, emotionally accommodated version of the most terrifying plane they have come up with.

    Some call you Hades. They say your world is divided into Tartarus for the worst transgressors, the Fields of Mourning for those wronged by love, and the Asphodel Meadows for the souls of the majority, and the Elysian Fields for the most virtuous, a place you could not set foot in.

    Being the lowest of the regions, the worst, most sinned humans and the enemies of your Father and cousins were to be sent to Tartarus. Kronos, the God of agriculture, driven by power had swallowed his own children to overturn the prophesy which declared that his downfall would be at the hands of his offspring was banished to your realm. You alone know the disgust and the pain of keeping a monster as him around you. You take solace in the fact that those who suffered by him endure it for a year to be then washed away by the Styx into a blissful nothing.

    There is the one you are truly living. Your throne of skulls and thorns, embedded with rubies as red as the blood of all those who have suffered there. The fires blaze golden around the floating island, spirits screaming from the depths of it. The courtroom with Beelzebub, Asmodeus and Purson gliding in their black robes with flies and moths circling them like the anti-halo. There’s Persephone’s throne, gold with engraved beryls and aquamarines. It’s empty because it’s spring on earth. Flowers you will never breathe. There’s a pristine skull at the base of the throne- a footstool. Your footstool. You are gliding in in your dark green gown, golden at the edges and the golden crown of thorns glimmering on your head. You sit on your throne, your hands on the armrests, your fingers pricked slightly by the thorns drawing blood ever so slightly. You sit up straight, your back taut with power and purpose. Time gazes intently into your face, for even she has not seen something quite so sad and beautiful, your blood red lips and your eyes closed. Eight seconds of ringing silence later, your eyes open sharply, revealing the dark in your irises. The never-ending pit of centuries of souls that have entered, suffering, lost, malicious, their humanity being stripped by their scalding screams. The pain that you have learnt to numb yourself to because you are the ruler and you have to perform your duty to maintain the ecological balance, even if it hollows you inside.

    You want to get up from the throne and dance on the needles that make up the floor of your court. You want to shout from the spires of your castle to all the tortured souls to join you in your dance, offer them respite. You want them to look at you with something other than pure fear. You want to plead at their feet, tears streaming your face, a face that they know not to be human or angel- begging them to understand that you are not evil or shallow. You want to show them that every time they scream, a part of you feels pain much worse than when your wings were ripped from you and you fell from heaven. Your eyes aren’t red with anger, they are stained with the blood of all who suffer, blood you could not wash off. The judgement you have had to make standing on the blurry line between good and bad has haunted you for centuries and it is a cycle that never ends. Robin hood may go to hell for thieving but Elysium waits for he who is remembered as the man who was charitable to the poor. You have tried to stop the trolley that carries the life of many against that of one. One innocent human. But it has been running frictionless on the track of cruel helplessness. And that trolley rolls its way into what humans believe happens to them after death because the only thing they like believing more than that they are meant for Asphodel at worst, is that they are immortal. That they are more than the ashes that cement over hundreds of years. They believe that those ashes will be the founding stone of the next civilization and they will play their part in that too. The part of them beyond the ashes goes to you to tide time till called upon. You try to warn them, you are screaming yourself hoarse telling them to keep away but they are just out of earshot.

    The seven deadly sins that flew out of innocent Pandora’s box were pride, sloth, greed, gluttony, wrath, envy and lust. You never wanted to let them out. You wanted to keep them buried under your floorboards forever, forgotten. These spirits that you let out have been precursors to every disaster, every heartbreak and is part of them. And yet, they like to believe that they are all not destined for eternal damnation. So do you, in futility. It isn’t blind faith that ties in them the belief that who they are beyond their vessels aren’t angry wraiths fighting their way away from you. They believe it because of that last spirit that glowed brightest in the end. Take comfort in that, oh doomed, beautiful one.
