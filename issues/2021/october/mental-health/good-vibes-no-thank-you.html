Good Vibes? No Thank You.: Toxic Positivity in a Nutshell

  • Mental Health   16/10/2021   Vaishali Sarah Mundle

Let’s play a little game:

Raise your hand if you have been told something along the lines of, “oh just be happy” when you’ve been low.

My hand is up high enough to break through the ceiling.

Many of us often rant to our close friends and family, more like, share what is bothering us and just get it all out of our system (which by the way, is the healthy way to go about things).

There is always this one friend, or family member- because let’s face it, we’ve all made poor decisions, who says: “you’ll get over it” or “stop being so negative!” In certain cases, one might even guilt trip you because what you are going through is probably “not as bad” as theirs or someone else’s.

Cardinal rule: You never, I repeat, NEVER invalidate someone’s feelings just because you have had it worse or you do not understand it.

It is already hard enough for someone to open up, imagine getting shut out by someone you trust only because they have either never gone through it, therefore do not understand it, or have felt it too but in a greater magnitude, therefore feeling like they have the supreme authority over that issue.

It is more than understandable if someone comes to you with their problems but you have no clue what to say. There’s a reason behind this: you try to find a solution to their problems or struggles.

No one can solve your problems for you therefore, you cannot solve others’ problems. Of course, that does not mean you should bottle up and not share at all. It is your life to live, in other words, you are the main character of your own story. It is you who powers through all your struggles, it is the one you confide in who cheers your way through it, not come to the field and do it for you.

Sometimes when someone vents, it means they just want someone to hear them out. They want to let it out and they want to feel all the emotions that they’re feeling. If someone confides in you, you hear them out and be there for them regardless of knowing what to say or not. If you do not know what to say, be honest about it and don’t cover it up with unnecessarily positive quotes you stole from the last Good Morning WhatsApp forward that you received.

All this said and done, one may just wonder, what could possibly go wrong if you said such things to someone. If you just said “be positive” to someone, like it never crossed their mind they’d probably never trust anyone enough to open up again. A few poorly chosen words could impact someone for life.

So next time perhaps, say something like “It’s normal and valid to feel this way, I know it’s tough and I’m here for you”.

If you are physically around them, give them a hug, be there for them but give them the space they need.
