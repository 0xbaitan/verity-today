The Mind and Body Connection

  • Mental Health   16/10/2021   Aarna Garg

Yung Pueblo once said, “We carry our attachments and pain in our bodies. As we let them go, our bodies change”. The intangible nature of emotion has been a subject of interest since the beginning of recorded history. The body achieves what the mind believes: the mind-body connection is the link between a person’s thoughts, attitudes, and behaviours and their physical health. Often when we focus on one, we ignore the other. However, we don’t realize the two are deeply intertwined.

Ancient philosophers to modern scientists have all tried to establish a connection between the two. It is essential to understand the connection and interaction between the body and the mind to be able to achieve good health and wellness. Being in a good mental state can keep you healthy and help prevent serious health conditions. A study found that positive psychological well-being can reduce the risks of poor physical health conditions. Physical health issues can arise from a negative psychological state, stress and anxiety can lead to high blood pressure and can eventually lead to serious heart conditions.

If you think that your emotions are burdening you and are at the root of physical health issues you are struggling with, practising therapies that can help relax the mind will also help relax the body. If you have been made to feel like the symptoms you are experiencing such as physical pain are just “in your head” because there is no injury or physical source it is definitely NOT just in your head. If you are struggling with stress, anxiety, depression a phenomenon called psychogenic pain can occur and it occurs when your pain is related to underlying psychological, emotional, or behavioural factors.

Naturally navigating and healing from emotions and symptoms that are negatively impacting your health is essential. The negative emotions or negative psychological state can be caused by daily stress, trauma, loss, heartache, and difficult life transitions. At any moment in life, if you are mentally exhausted or facing any mental health issues, approaching help or doing things to relax your mind is essential. Whenever you are overexerting yourself, the mind gives us signals via the body such as feeling sore thus, maintaining a balance between the body and mind can help resolve tiredness and exhaustion.

Our mind and body need time to rebuild and rejuvenate since the mind and body therapies are heavily interrelated. Yoga, meditation, cognitive-behaviour therapy and creative art therapy like music, dance and arts itself can help freshen up your mind and body. This helps us think clearly and perceive things well while feeling our best.
