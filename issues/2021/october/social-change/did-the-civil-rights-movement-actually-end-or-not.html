The Ongoing Fight Against Racial Discrimination

  • Social Change   16/10/2021   Aarna Garg

Even after years of fighting for rights, protesting, giving speeches and holding rallies throughout the nations, the discrimination and segregation against people of colour still hasn’t come to an end. The Covid-19 pandemic has raised racist and xenophobic violence and discrimination against Asians and people of Asian descent. On May 8, 2020, United Nations Secretary-General Antonio Guterres said that “the pandemic continues to unleash a tsunami of hate and xenophobia, scapegoating and scaremongering” and urged governments to “act now to strengthen the immunity of our societies against the virus of hate.” Racial discrimination has been prominent since the 16th century. While the civil rights movement may have ended, the fight for civil rights did not end.

Racism is a holistic issue that impacts people economically, socially, politically, and even psychologically. Due to racial discrimination, people of colour are often given fewer opportunities, which degrades the standard of living due to financial instability. Often, the opinions and voices of the coloured community are oppressed. Recently, there was a major case of police brutality, where George Floyd, a black man, was murdered by a white police officer, under suspicion of his using a counterfeit $20 bill. It was simply brutal and unjust as the officers refused to hear him out.

To fight the issue of racial discrimination, it is important that we raise awareness, protest, teach kids to love, promote peace, report unjust treatment and support organizations that are working towards combatting racism such as the United Nations Human Rights Council, and more. Together, we must continue the fight for equality which Nelson Mandela, Martin Luther King, Gandhi, and many other activists started.

Racism and racial discrimination are witnessed in employment, education, housing and access to social services, in the form of hostile attitudes, stigmatization, and human rights violations against people of colour. In the future, in every nation, all races should be equal and people should be given equal opportunities regardless of their colour, race, religion and background. I hope that we are free from the manacles of racism and prejudice. Everyone should voice their opinions, fight for their rights and not let anyone oppress them, because every individual is of an equal moral worth and must be given equal opportunities, as the colour of their skin does not affect capability.
