Marriage Is Not A Certificate of Sexual Consent

With reference to Exodus 20:17 which states, and I quote, "You shall not covet your neighbor's house. You shall not covet your neighbor's wife, or his manservant or maidservant, his ox or donkey, or anything that belongs to your neighbor".

This can be used as clear evidence, as mentioned by Robert Kawashima, of how women have been legally labelled as objects belonging to men since they're on the list of "things" that people shouldn't covet, with homes and servants along with them.

Robert told VICE: "The basic shocking principle in my view is that women were seen as a type of property". The ancients did not have a proper legally-binding law against forcible rape, but presented either the husband or father of the deceased victim as a victim of the same case. In the ancient Babylonian code of Hammurabi, women did not have a separate social status; the only one allotted to them was in relation to men, that is, their father or husband. Under the very first laws of rape, the rape of a virgin was liable to a death sentence, whereby the survivor was not to be blamed, but when a married woman was raped, both survivor and the guilty were punished by drowning, and blamed equally.

First, the man was shown as the victim of the case, and then the married woman was blamed for the misfortune of such a dreadful event. The Manusmriti imposes several restrictions upon the activities of women in public, one of which prohibits a married woman from speaking to other men or meeting them without the presence of a responsible male family member. This is allegedly to protect them from other men in light of temptations or sexual advances.

This provides evidence that men have not been taught to control their sexual desires from the beginning of time, but have made sure with biased laws and regulations that women may be dominated and oppressed throughout the course of her life. Unlike women, men weren't bound to not seek sexual pleasure outside of their own marriage. In such cases they would only require mutual consent.

However,there were no laws against the men who violated their wife's consent, because rape was apparently not possible in a marriage, where the wife is portrayed as an object belonging to the husband and not as a separate identity. In the Laws written in the 11th and 12th century, where rape appeared not as a property crime but a violent crime against an individual, the exception of men raping their own women still existed.

In the words of Sir Matthew Hale, a notable English barrister of the 17th century, "the husband cannot be guilty of the rape committed by himself upon his lawful wife, for by their mutual matrimonial consent and contract the wife hath given up herself in this kind into her husband, which she cannot retract." In simple words, Hale sought women to be a man's property.

With rape beginning to be perceived as a threat to women outside of the household, the Indian Penal Code of 1860 was drafted, stating the exceptions to Section 375 (which deals with rape), "Sexual Intercourse by a man with his own wife who is above the age of 18, is not sexual assault".

However, we have fortunately seen certain progressive changes in the law:

(i) Second amendment of The Criminal Law, 1983 - whoever, being the husband or relative of the husband of a woman, subjects such women to cruelty shall be punished with imprisonment, wherein the word "cruelty" refers to any wilful conduct of a nature which would drive the woman to commit self-harm or cause grave injury or danger of life, limb or health (mental or physical) of the woman and harassment of woman. (ii) Amendment to India Evidence Act, 2002 - provided that in a prosecution of rape or attempt to commit rape it shall not be permissible to put questions in the cross examination of the prosecutrix as to her general immoral character. (iii) Protection of Children from Sexual Offenses Act, 2012 - provides protection of children from offenses of sexual assaults, sexual harassment and pornography while safeguarding the interests of the child at every stage. (iv) Criminal Law Act, 2013- provides punishment against sex crimes, redefines rape and made punishments more stringent including death for repeat rape offenders.

Meanwhile, in 2018, the court heard petitions filed by the NGO RIT Foundation and All India Democratic Women’s Association challenging the constitutionality of The Indian Penal Code 1980, section 375, and claiming that it discriminates against married women. While the Delhi HC agreed to examine the issues raised by advocate Karuna Nundy, the Delhi government claimed that striking off the exception would be an offense. The Centre stated that it would lead to the destabilisation of the institution of marriage, and become an easy tool for harassing husbands.

This notion hearkens back to the idea that a woman is nothing but a man's object after marriage, as well as his sexual property. Through various sources of entertainment, TV shows and documentaries, we are presented with the idea that the threat of rape solely originates outsold the household. Sexual Consent is the fundamental right of any woman, yet in India, the law protectors continue to uphold a man's right to rape his wife.

However, all hope is not lost, as a division bench of the Kerala High Court declared marital rape to be a ground for divorce .The purpose of feminist movements against the marital rape law is not only to scrape off the outdated mindset of perceiving a woman as a property of her husband, but also fighting to view women as individuals of their own entities. It is about challenging the larger rape culture which denies our mothers, sisters, daughters and wives the basic rights, respect and consent.
