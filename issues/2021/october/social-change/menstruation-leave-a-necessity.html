Menstruation Leave - A Necessity?

  • Social Change   16/10/2021   Nysa Malhotra

While many individuals believe that people should be entitled to a menstruation leave, it is also well-debated that making menstruation leave a necessity would simply result in “gendering” of the workplace.

Through further surveys, it was found that most people said that if individuals cannot work on certain days due to their menstrual cycle, then they should opt for some days off under a medical leave. Existence of a separate menstruation leave should not be in question. Further, it was said that not all people during “that time of the month” are totally unable to work with extreme pain. If, at all, a person has a particularly debilitating period, or is in a situation where they cannot work, then that should qualify under the larger bracket of medical leave. Some say that a menstruation leave would just elevate the concept of the patriarchy, allegedly proving that non-menstruators are more dependable & reliable.

However, we must not forget that everything is not as simple as it seems. Some deal with unbearable pain in the form of cramps & comparatively low health while on their periods, making it nearly impossible to work productively. Moreover, just because some people may not be in too much pain while on their periods does not negate the fact that others consider period leaves a necessity. Apart from this, we are aware of the fact that for some, periods are extremely painful, which therefore hinders their ability to work to their full potential. Moreover, all our bodies are built differently and each body responds to things differently, and has different needs which they need to cater to. Therefore, when we speak of periods, we must take into consideration the concept of period leave.

Previously, when the chief executive officer of Zomato said that their female employees could take a leave of ten days a year without having to give any explanation for the same, it sparked violent debates. Conservatives were seen arguing against liberals, men were sparring with women, and the patriarchy was fighting feminism. However, what was surprising was that even women engaged in great debate against other women.

Countries like Japan, South Korea, and China grant period leaves: Japan had even passed menstruation leave policies in 1947. We cannot progress as a society or nation unless we learn to accept changes and understand the problems of our fellow citizens. Moreover, what is of utmost importance is that this should not even have been a debatable topic in the first place, considering how much pain almost all people go through while on their periods. Therefore, only when we discard this regressive mindset can we try to understand the problems, and thus find a solution to the same.
