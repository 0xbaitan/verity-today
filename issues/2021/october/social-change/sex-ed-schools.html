Sex Cognizance and Its Stigma

  • Social Change   16/10/2021   Saranya Mondal

Four out of ten high school students reportedly have sex.

Almost every parent is concerned about their child’s safety and naturally resorts to protecting them. Educating them is one of those many ways.

Due to the stigma built around intercourse as a whole, people tend to discourage having “the talk”, which may cause the curious youth to look for guidance from incorrect and misleading sources.

Sex education does not only comprise of the knowledge about intercourse but it is also inclusive of, albeit not restricted to, puberty, the human anatomy, safety measures, maintaining hygiene, awareness about sexual orientation and the LGBTQ+ community, abuse and consent.

One of the many issues faced due to the lack of sex education is the risk of teen pregnancy. Sexual education has proven to decrease birth rates and extend the average time before initial intercourse among students. This form of education introduces students to the idea of an abortion, its process, and even STDs.

In fact, Mississippi’s sexual educators need state approval to teach about STDs, and do not include a curriculum on condoms; which is why, according to the 2018 study conducted by the Centre for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), they have the highest rates of Gonorrhea in the U.S. This is why STDs are higher in states that have incomplete or no access to sex education.

Avoiding sexual education will lead to the possibility of unsafe sex or even sex oriented abuse. Most perpetrators are ignorant, and it is mandatory to educate children during their formative years so that they grow up to respect consent and not condone any of these misfortunate happenings in their environment. The lack of sex education leads to nothing but loss.

Bullying is one of these many losses. The exposure to incorrect sources of pornography creates an idea which contradicts reality and leads to unrealistic societal expectations. The absence of vaginal hair is one such “beauty standard”, causing people to take unhygienic measures to get rid of it. All these expectations have now turned into “norms”, and are harmful both physically and mentally.

Apart from achieving personal knowledge, this helps acquire a positive attitude towards sexual and reproductive health and behaviors. We develop a better understanding as to what is considered a healthy and an unhealthy relationship: to freely communicate, and not avoid speaking up, and to value, understand and exercise autonomy over our own bodies.

Period poverty caused due to the lack of awareness, and those who support the recent Texan abortion ban are just the consequence of the dearth of sex education.

It’s time we speak up for the talk that most of us are deprived of. Being dress-coded unnecessarily, slut-shamed, and being blamed as the victim are the slew of issues that have to be avoided. To overcome them, implementing sex education in all schools and mandating it as much as possible should be a viable option.

Attaining gender equality, being safe, and having control over our own bodies while having basic human rights is only possible if we are aware. Ignorance may be just as serious an issue as the negative beliefs around how “impure” something as normal and regular as intercourse is.
